Veterinary anatomy overview

Thoracic vertebrae form the dorsal thoracic wall and articulate with ribs.

Ox: Usually 13. Cranial spinous processes contribute to the withers. The anticlinal vertebra varies, commonly T11-T13 in cattle rather than being fixed at T13. Typical vertebrae bear costal foveae for rib heads and transverse costal foveae for rib tubercles; first and last ribs have modified articulations.

Detailed anatomy

THORACIC VERTEBRAE

Cattle usually have 13 thoracic vertebrae. Typical bodies carry cranial/caudal costal foveae for rib heads and transverse processes carry foveae for rib tubercles, with modifications at the first and last ribs. Cranial spinous processes form the withers. The anticlinal vertebra is variable in cattle (about T11-T13); it is commonly about T16 in the horse and T11 in dog/cat. Do not use T13 as an invariant bovine landmark.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

18 thoracic vertebrae. Anticlinal = T16. Withers very prominent.

Dog

13. Anticlinal = T11. Spines shorter, more uniform.

Pig

14-15 (variable). Spines flat and broad.

Fowl

T1-T5 fused into NOTARIUM. Last thoracic fused with synsacrum. No flexible thoracic spine.

Clinical relevance

Fistulous Withers (Cattle/Horse): Infection of the supraspinous bursa over T2-T5 spines from yoke/saddle pressure. Causes a draining wound on top of shoulders.
