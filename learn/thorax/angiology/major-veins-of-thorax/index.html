Veterinary anatomy overview

Cranial and caudal venae cavae return systemic venous blood to the right atrium; pulmonary veins return oxygenated blood to the left atrium. Important azygos patterns are species-specific: cattle and sheep commonly retain a left azygos draining to the coronary sinus, pigs also commonly retain a left azygos, while horses and dogs commonly retain a right azygos draining toward the cranial vena cava.

Detailed anatomy

MAJOR THORACIC VEINS

The cranial vena cava drains head, neck, forelimbs and cranial trunk; the caudal vena cava passes through the caval foramen to the right atrium. Azygos veins drain the dorsal thoracic wall. Cattle/sheep and pigs commonly have a left azygos entering the coronary sinus; horses and dogs commonly have a right azygos entering the cranial vena cava. Pulmonary veins return oxygenated blood to the left atrium; the coronary sinus drains the heart wall to the right atrium.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Commonly a left azygos draining into the coronary sinus.

Horse

RIGHT azygos only.

Dog

Commonly a right azygos draining into the cranial vena cava.

Pig

LEFT azygos only.

Fowl

Two cranial venae cavae (right & left) — both open separately into right atrium.

Clinical relevance

Cranial vena-cava obstruction: May cause venous distension and edema of the head, neck, forelimbs or brisket. Bottle jaw specifically means submandibular edema and is classically associated with hypoproteinemia; it is not a synonym for brisket edema.
