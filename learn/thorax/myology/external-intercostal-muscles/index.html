Veterinary anatomy overview

Type: Inspiratory muscles of thoracic wall

Origin: Caudal border of each rib

Insertion: Cranial border of next (caudal) rib

Fibre Direction: Caudo-VENTRAL (downwards & backwards — like fingers in pocket)

Function: Pull ribs forward and outward → ENLARGE thorax → INSPIRATION.

Extent: Fill the intercostal spaces from vertebrae to costochondral junction.

Detailed anatomy

EXTERNAL INTERCOSTAL MUSCLES

External intercostals occupy the interosseous spaces and generally elevate ribs during inspiration. Fibers run caudoventrally and do not extend through the interchondral region to the sternum. Segmental thoracic ventral branches supply them; T1 also contributes to the brachial plexus and the last thoracic ventral branch is subcostal/costoabdominal rather than a typical intercostal nerve.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Same arrangement; very important during exercise breathing.

Dog

Less developed; diaphragm does most of the breathing work.

Clinical relevance

Intercostal nerve block: Local anaesthetic placed at the caudal border of the rib (where vessels & nerve run) blocks pain from rib fractures or from chest wall surgery.
