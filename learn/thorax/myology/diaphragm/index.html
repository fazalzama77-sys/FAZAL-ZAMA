Veterinary anatomy overview

Type: The MAIN muscle of breathing (chief inspiratory muscle).

Shape: Dome-shaped sheet between thorax and abdomen.

Parts:
- Muscular periphery: Costal, Sternal, Lumbar (Crura) parts.
- Tendinous Centre (Centrum tendineum): Y-shaped central tendon.

Three Holes (very important):
1. Aortic hiatus (most dorsal, in lumbar part) — passes Aorta, Azygos vein, Thoracic duct.
2. Oesophageal hiatus (middle) — passes Oesophagus + Vagus trunks.
3. Caval foramen (most ventral, in tendinous centre) — passes Caudal Vena Cava.

Function: Contracts → flattens → enlarges thorax → INSPIRATION.

Nerve: Phrenic nerve (C5-C7).

Detailed anatomy

DIAPHRAGM

The diaphragm is the principal inspiratory muscle. Its costal, sternal and lumbar parts converge on a central tendon. Contraction flattens the dome and enlarges the thorax. The aortic hiatus transmits the aorta and associated structures, the oesophageal hiatus transmits oesophagus and vagal trunks, and the caval foramen transmits the caudal vena cava. Phrenic nerves provide motor supply. Avoid unsourced fixed percentages for its contribution, which varies with species, posture and respiratory state.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Very strong; right crus is huge and partly attached to last ribs (helps in galloping rhythm — feet hit ground in time with diaphragm push).

Dog

The diaphragm is the principal inspiratory muscle; relative contribution varies with posture and respiratory demand.

Pig

Right crus very large; oesophageal hiatus relatively narrow.

Fowl

No mammalian muscular diaphragm. Coordinated sternocostal and body-wall movements ventilate air sacs, which drive airflow through rigid lungs.

Clinical relevance

Diaphragmatic Hernia: A tear in the diaphragm (e.g. after road accident in dog, or after calving in cattle) lets abdominal organs slip into the chest — causes severe breathing difficulty.
Phrenic nerve injury: Paralyses one half of the diaphragm — that side moves up instead of down during breathing (paradoxical movement). 'Hiccups' = repeated jerky contractions of the diaphragm.
