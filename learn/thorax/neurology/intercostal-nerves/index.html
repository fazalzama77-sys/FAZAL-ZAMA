Veterinary anatomy overview

Ventral branches of thoracic spinal nerves supply the thoracic wall. Typical intercostal nerves accompany intercostal vessels near caudal rib borders; the last thoracic ventral branch is termed the subcostal/costoabdominal nerve, and T1 also contributes to the brachial plexus. Intercostal blockade targets the nerve at a defined level using procedure-specific landmarks.

Detailed anatomy

INTERCOSTAL NERVES

Most ventral branches of thoracic spinal nerves run with intercostal vessels near caudal rib borders and give muscular and cutaneous branches. T1 also contributes to the brachial plexus, and the last thoracic ventral branch is subcostal/costoabdominal rather than a typical intercostal nerve. Intercostal blocks place anesthetic near the target nerve at the selected level; safe needle trajectory depends on the specific procedure and anatomy.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

18 pairs — same arrangement; commonly blocked for rib fracture pain.

Dog

13 pairs; intercostal block is routine analgesia after thoracotomy.

Clinical relevance

Needle safety: The principal intercostal neurovascular bundle is near the caudal rib border. Select the exact rib border, depth and direction for the intended block, thoracocentesis or surgical approach rather than applying one rule without procedural context.
