Veterinary anatomy overview

Overview:
• Four-compartment stomach: rumen, reticulum, omasum and abomasum. Rumen and reticulum form the functional ruminoreticulum; this list should not be labelled a simple topographic order.
• Rumen is the largest (up to 150L in adult cow) — acts as a fermentation vat.
• Reticulum has a honeycomb mucosal pattern — also called the 'hardware stomach'.
• Omasum (many-plies / psalterium) filters particles; Abomasum is the true glandular stomach.

Detailed anatomy

The Complex Gastric Apparatus of Ruminants

Ruminants are characterized by a massive composite stomach that dominates the left and ventral abdominal cavity. The first three compartments (proventriculi) are non-glandular and lined with stratified squamous epithelium, while the abomasum is glandular and represents the homolog of the simple stomach.

Reticulum: The most cranial compartment, pressed against the diaphragm mainly left of the median plane and under cover of approximately the 6th-8th ribs. Its mucosa has a honeycomb pattern. The ruminoreticular fold partly separates it from the rumen, with which it communicates widely.

Rumen: Occupies the entire left side of the abdominal cavity, from diaphragm to pelvis. Divided by muscular pillars (coronary, longitudinal, accessory) into ventral and dorsal sacs. Site of primary fermentation by microflora generating VFAs. Gas accumulation causes bloat.

Omasum: On the right side, spherical with leaf-like folds (laminae omasi) that trap and squeeze digesta. The omasal canal connects to the abomasum via the omasoabomasal orifice.

Abomasum: The 'true' stomach — glandular, secretes HCl and pepsin. Located on the ventral abdominal floor, right of midline. Can undergo displacement (LDA/RDA) in high-producing dairy cows.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Four-compartment stomach. Adult rumen capacity and saliva production vary with body size and diet. The reticular/oesophageal groove reflex directs suckled milk toward the abomasum in the calf.

Horse

Simple single-compartment stomach of relatively small capacity (approximately 8-15 L depending on body size and source). Vomiting is extremely difficult because of the powerful cardia/lower oesophageal sphincter, acute oesophageal entry angle and associated anatomy—not because of the pyloric sphincter. The cecum and ascending colon support hindgut fermentation.

Pig

Monogastric with a well-developed diverticulum gastri. Gastric ulcers common near pars oesophagea.

Dog

Simple stomach. Prone to gastric dilatation-volvulus (GDV) — stomach rotates on its axis, trapping gas. Emergency.

Clinical relevance

Left displacement of abomasum: The gas-filled abomasum moves between the rumen and left abdominal wall, producing a characteristic percussion-auscultation ping. Right displacement/volvulus: Volvulus compromises blood supply and is an emergency. Ruminal tympany: May be frothy or free-gas; free-gas tympany has several causes and is not synonymous with vagal indigestion. Vagal indigestion: Functional outflow failure produces variable abdominal distension patterns depending on the affected compartment and stage.
