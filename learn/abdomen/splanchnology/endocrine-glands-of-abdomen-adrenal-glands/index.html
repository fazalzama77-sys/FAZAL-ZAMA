Veterinary anatomy overview

High-Yield Points:
• Paired, retroperitoneal, on craniomedial pole of each kidney.
• Cortex (outer, mesodermal): zona glomerulosa (mineralocorticoids — aldosterone), zona fasciculata (glucocorticoids — cortisol), zona reticularis (sex steroids).
• Medulla (inner, neuroectodermal — modified sympathetic ganglion): chromaffin cells → adrenaline + noradrenaline.
• Right adrenal: more cranial, often heart-shaped in ox.
• Left adrenal: bean-shaped, related to the floating left kidney.
• Other endocrine in abdomen: pancreatic islets (already covered).

Detailed anatomy

Adrenal (Suprarenal) Glands

Each adrenal gland is a small flattened organ (~5 cm × 2 cm in cattle) lying retroperitoneally on the cranio-medial pole of its kidney. They are made of two embryologically and functionally distinct parts:

Cortex (mesodermal origin) — produces steroid hormones in three concentric zones:
• Zona glomerulosa (outer) → aldosterone (regulates Na+/K+, blood pressure).
• Zona fasciculata (middle, thickest) → cortisol (glucocorticoid; stress response).
• Zona reticularis (inner) → adrenal androgens.

Medulla (neural-crest derived and functionally comparable to a modified sympathetic ganglion) — chromaffin cells secrete epinephrine/adrenaline and norepinephrine/noradrenaline. Their relative proportions vary by species and physiological state; a fixed 80:20 ratio is not a universal veterinary rule.

Vasculature: The glands receive multiple arterial branches from adjacent major vessels. Venous drainage is asymmetric and species-dependent; the right adrenal vein commonly has a short connection to the caudal vena cava, while left-sided drainage commonly joins the renal or adjacent venous system.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Right gland heart-shaped, left bean-shaped. Cortex pale yellow; medulla reddish-brown.

Horse

Larger and more elongated. Strong cortisol response to stress (laminitis link).

Dog

Small, bean-shaped. Common diseases: hyperadrenocorticism (Cushing's) and hypoadrenocorticism (Addison's).

Clinical relevance

Cushing's Disease (HAC): Excess cortisol → polyuria/polydipsia, pot belly, alopecia (especially old dogs). Addison's Disease: Adrenal insufficiency — collapse, hyperkalemia. Pheochromocytoma: Catecholamine-secreting medullary tumour.
