Veterinary anatomy overview

High-Yield Points:
• Largest lymphoid organ. Stores blood, filters old RBCs, immune surveillance.
• Ox spleen: Flat, oblong, bluish-grey. Lies between the LEFT side of the rumen and the diaphragm — under the last few ribs (10th-13th) on the LEFT side.
• Held by gastrosplenic + phrenicosplenic + renosplenic ligaments.
• Has a hilus on visceral surface for vessels (splenic artery from celiac).
• Splenectomy site (cattle): Left dorsal flank, between last 2 ribs.

Detailed anatomy

The Bovine Spleen

The spleen is a flat, elongated, oblong organ measuring ~50 × 12 cm in the adult cow. Its colour is bluish-grey to brown. It lies entirely on the left side of the abdomen, sandwiched between the dorsal sac of the rumen (visceral surface) and the diaphragm (parietal surface), under cover of the last few ribs (approximately 10th to 13th).

Attachments: Gastrosplenic ligament (to rumen), phrenicosplenic ligament (to diaphragm), renosplenic ligament (to left kidney). The spleen does not contact the left abdominal wall directly because of the rumen.

Vessels: Splenic artery (from celiac artery) and splenic vein (drains into portal system).

Functions: Storage of erythrocytes (especially in horse), removal of senescent RBCs, hematopoiesis in fetal life, antigen filtration via white pulp.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Flat, oblong, dorsally placed on left under last ribs. Hilus near dorsal border.

Horse

Sickle/triangular shape. Very large reservoir of RBCs (~12 L). Located in left flank — palpable per rectum.

Dog

Elongated tongue-shape; very mobile. Common site of hemangiosarcoma in older large breeds (Golden Retriever, GSD).

Pig

Long, narrow, strap-like spleen.

Clinical relevance

Splenectomy: Indicated for ruptured spleen, splenic torsion, or neoplasia. Left flank approach in cattle. Splenic Rupture (dog): Hemangiosarcoma rupture → acute abdomen + collapse. Anthrax: Spleen massively enlarged, soft, tar-like — DO NOT cut open suspected anthrax carcasses.
