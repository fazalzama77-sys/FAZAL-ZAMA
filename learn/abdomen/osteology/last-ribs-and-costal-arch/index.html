Veterinary anatomy overview

High-Yield Points:
• Caudal ribs (10-13 in ox) form the cranial-lateral wall of the abdomen.
• 13th rib in ox = the last of five asternal ribs and normally contributes to the costal arch; a supernumerary 14th rib, when present, may be floating.
• Costal arch = continuous arc of cartilages of asternal ribs (9-13) joining together → forms the caudal margin of the thorax / cranial limit of flank.
• Cranial limit of paralumbar fossa = last rib.
• Liver biopsy in cow: 11th right intercostal space on a horizontal line from tuber coxae.

Detailed anatomy

RIBS BORDERING THE ABDOMEN

The cranial part of the abdominal cavity is overlapped by the caudal thoracic skeleton. In the ox, ribs 9-13 are asternal (their cartilages do not reach the sternum directly but join into a single 'costal arch'). The 13th rib is normally the last asternal rib and contributes to the costal arch; an occasional supernumerary 14th rib may end freely as a floating rib.

The costal arch forms a continuous concave ridge arching from the sternum cranio-ventrally up to the last rib dorso-caudally — it marks the cranial boundary of the abdomen externally and the caudal boundary of the thoracic cage. The diaphragm attaches here; thus organs nominally 'abdominal' (liver, cranial rumen, reticulum) actually lie deep to the caudal ribs and are protected by them.

Clinical landmarks on the costal arch:
• Right 11th ICS (on a line from tuber coxae) = Liver biopsy site in adult cow.
• Right 7th-8th ICS = approximate area for percussion of liver dullness.
• Left 6th ICS, lower third = Cardiac auscultation (apex).
• Right 11th-13th ICS = approach to gall bladder (cranial to liver biopsy).

Comparative anatomy

Horse

18 pairs of ribs (8 sternal + 10 asternal). Last rib floating. Long costal arch.

Dog

13 pairs (9 sternal + 4 asternal). 13th rib floating.

Pig

14-15 pairs of ribs (variable) — among highest in domestic species.

Fowl

7 pairs of ribs; ribs are jointed with vertebral and sternal parts. Uncinate processes hook caudally on each rib for muscular attachment.

Clinical relevance

Liver Biopsy (Cow): 11th right ICS — count from last rib (13th) cranially. Costal Cartilage Fracture: Trauma → painful swelling along arch. Splenic sampling in cattle: should be ultrasound-guided and planned from confirmed splenic topography; a blind 'last left ICS' puncture should not be taught as a routine landmark.
