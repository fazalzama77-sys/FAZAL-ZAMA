Dog vs. Horse

Supratrochlear Foramen: Veterinary Anatomy Explained | IVRI

Why this anatomy matters

A hole in the distal humerus found in Dogs. It allows the anconeal process of the ulna to snap deeply into the humerus, permitting extreme elbow extension required for the long stride of a sprinting predator. In Horses, this area is solid bone to support the massive vertical weight of the column.

Functional explanation

The Dog's elbow is like a folding knife with a notch cut out so the blade can open 180 degrees. The Horse's elbow is like a solid steel pillar—it can't fold as far, but it can hold up a building.

Clinical relevance

Radiographic interpretation: The foramen appears as a lucent (dark) zone in dogs which is normal, but a similar look in large animals would suggest a cyst or fracture.
