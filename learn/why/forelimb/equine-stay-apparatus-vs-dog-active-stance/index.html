Horse vs. Dog

Why this anatomy matters

Horses have a STAY APPARATUS — a passive locking system of ligaments + check ligaments + intermediate tubercle + suspensory apparatus — that lets them stand and even sleep on their feet with NO muscle effort. Dogs lack this — they must actively use muscles to stand and lie down to rest. Energy efficiency vs flexibility trade-off.

Functional explanation

Horse legs are like camera tripods that lock open — once set, they hold the load with zero energy. Dog legs are like collapsible umbrellas — they need active muscle to stay up.

Clinical relevance

Recumbent horse warning: If a healthy adult horse lies down for more than an hour, it's a clinical red flag (pain, illness, exhaustion). Horses are physically built to stand. Decubitus ulcers in dogs: Common in old/recumbent dogs that can't reposition themselves.
