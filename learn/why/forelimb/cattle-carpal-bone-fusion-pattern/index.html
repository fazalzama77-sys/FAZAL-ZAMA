Why this anatomy matters

Number of carpal bones differs by species: Ox = 6 (C2+C3 fused, C1 absent), Horse = 7-8, Dog = 7 (radial+intermediate fused into 'intermedioradial'), Pig = 8 (full complete pattern). Fusion reduces friction surfaces + adds strength for weight-bearing — characteristic of cursorial (running) species.

Functional explanation

Single-digit and cloven-foot runners (horse, ox, sheep) have fewer carpal bones — fusion + loss reduce friction surfaces. Dog and pig retain more — they need rotational paw use.

Clinical relevance

Slab fracture of C3 (horse): Common race-horse injury from repetitive concussion on the carpus. Radiographic identification is essential. Carpal hygroma (cow): Bursitis over precarpal area — chronic 'kneeling' on hard concrete causes a precarpal subcutaneous bursa.
