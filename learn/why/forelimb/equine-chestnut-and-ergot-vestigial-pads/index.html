Why this anatomy matters

Horses have two small horny patches on each leg: the CHESTNUT (medial side, above the carpus on forelimb or below the hock on hindlimb) and the ERGOT (caudal aspect of fetlock). These are small vestigial horny remnants of the carpal/metacarpal pad region. In the modern single-digit horse foot, they persist as these non-functional patches.

Functional explanation

Like the appendix or wisdom teeth in humans — leftover hardware from when the body was used differently.

Clinical relevance

Chestnut patterns are unique to each horse — used like fingerprints for identification (chestnut + ergot pictures on passports). Not generally affected by disease but can crack and need trimming.
