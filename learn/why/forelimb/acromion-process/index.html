Ox vs. Horse

Why this anatomy matters

The Acromion serves as a lever arm for the deltoid muscle to aid in abduction (moving limb away from body). The Horse, specialized for speed, moves strictly in the sagittal plane; lateral movement is a liability. Thus, the horse lacks the acromion, resulting in a streamlined shoulder. The Ox possesses it for stability on uneven terrain.

Functional explanation

The Acromion is like a side-handle on a machine; it gives you leverage to pull things sideways. The Horse is a drag racer—it only goes straight. The design omits the side-handle to reduce drag and weight.

Clinical relevance

Palpation landmark: The acromion is palpable in the ox and dog but absent in the horse. This changes the location of the palpable 'point of the shoulder'.
