Horse vs. Ox

Distal Check Ligament: Veterinary Anatomy Explained | IVRI

Why this anatomy matters

A robust safety cable in the Horse connecting the carpus to the Deep Digital Flexor Tendon. It protects the muscle belly from tearing under the massive load of a single digit and assists in the Stay Apparatus. It is rudimentary or absent in the Ox, which splits the load over two digits.

Functional explanation

Think of a tow truck cable. The Check Ligament is a secondary safety chain attached to the main cable. If the load gets too heavy, the safety chain takes the strain so the winch motor (muscle) doesn't burn out.

Clinical relevance

'Check Ligament Desmotomy' is a surgical procedure performed in horses to treat club foot (flexural deformity); this is not performed in cattle.
