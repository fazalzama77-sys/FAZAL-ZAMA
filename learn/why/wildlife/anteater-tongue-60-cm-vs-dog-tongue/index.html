Giant Anteater vs. Dog

Why this anatomy matters

The giant anteater has a tongue ~60 cm long — longer than its head — anchored not to the hyoid like in mammals but extending all the way back to the sternum. It can flick in/out up to 150 times per minute. Dog tongue, by comparison, is muscular and short, used for lapping water and panting. Anteater design = specialist termite-eating tool.

Functional explanation

The dog tongue is a wet washcloth. The anteater tongue is a sticky retractable measuring tape that can reach 60 cm into a termite mound.

Clinical relevance

Captive anteaters need specialised feeding (puréed insectivore diet through narrow tubes) because their toothless tongue mechanism can't handle pellets. Tongue injury or atrophy = starvation.
