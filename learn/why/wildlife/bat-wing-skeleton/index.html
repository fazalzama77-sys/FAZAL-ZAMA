Bat vs. Dog

Why this anatomy matters

The Bat's forelimb is radically modified for flight: digits II–V are massively elongated to support a thin double-layered skin membrane (patagium) that forms the wing. The thumb (digit I) remains short with a claw. The dog forelimb retains the standard 5 short digits for ground locomotion.

Functional explanation

The Dog's forelimb is a normal hand. The Bat took the same hand, stretched the fingers to umbrella-spoke length, and pulled a kite skin between them. Same parts — radically different function.

Clinical relevance

Wing-membrane tears in bats compromise thermoregulation and flight; healing is poor. Canine paw injuries are routine to repair.
