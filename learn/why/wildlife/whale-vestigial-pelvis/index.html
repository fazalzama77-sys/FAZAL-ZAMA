Whale/Dolphin vs. Dog

Whale Vestigial Pelvis: Veterinary Anatomy Explained | IVRI

Why this anatomy matters

Cetaceans (whales, dolphins) have lost their hindlimbs entirely and retain only a pair of small 'floating' pelvic bones suspended in muscle, with NO connection to the vertebral column. They serve only as anchor points for reproductive musculature. The dog pelvis, in contrast, is the fixed weight-bearing platform of the hindlimb.

Functional explanation

The Dog's pelvis is the chassis of a car. The Whale kept the chassis bolts but threw away the wheels, axle and frame — leaving just two stubs floating uselessly inside the body.

Clinical relevance

Vestigial pelvic bones can be felt on dissection of stranded cetaceans. These bones serve as anchor points for reproductive musculature and are a notable example of structural variation across species.
