Why this anatomy matters

The American/Indian Bison has dramatically ELONGATED spinous processes on T2–T8 (some over 30 cm long), forming the famous shoulder hump. They serve as a long lever arm for the powerful nuchal-supraspinous ligament that supports the massive head used to plough snow for grazing. Domestic ox has short, normal-length spines.

Functional explanation

The Ox has a regular roof beam over its shoulders. The Bison has a CRANE TOWER built up there — the long spines provide the leverage needed to swing a massive head through deep snow without exhausting the neck muscles.

Clinical relevance

In bison, head-injury / wither-region trauma is more dangerous because of the extensive ligamentous attachments along these tall spines.
