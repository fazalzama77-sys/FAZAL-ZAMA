Why this anatomy matters

Sloths hang upside-down from branches using CURVED HOOK CLAWS that don't actively grip — they passively lock the digit closed via tendon engineering, so the sloth uses ZERO muscle energy to hang. Combined with the lowest metabolism of any mammal (~40% of expected for body weight), sloths can survive on a leaf diet that no other mammal could.

Functional explanation

A sloth's claws are like coat hooks — bend it once and it stays. No muscle needed to hold the coat. That's why a sloth can sleep upside-down for 15 hours a day.

Clinical relevance

Sloths in captivity often die of stress + improper diet. Heart rate can drop to ~15 bpm during rest — heart-rate-based anaesthesia monitors give false alarms. Body temperature varies more than other mammals.

Self-check question
