Why this anatomy matters

Antlers (deer family — Cervidae) are pure BONE, branched, shed and regrown ANNUALLY, and grow encased in nourishing 'velvet' skin. Horns (cattle, sheep, goats — Bovidae) are PERMANENT bony cores covered in keratin sheath that grows continuously. Antlers are the fastest growing bone known (up to 2.5 cm/day).

Functional explanation

Horns are like a permanent steel helmet — slow, permanent, always on. Antlers are like seasonal tournament armour — grown for one rutting season, then discarded and rebuilt fresh next year.

Clinical relevance

Dehorning (cattle) cuts living horn-and-bone — bleeding and pain. Velvet harvesting from deer is also invasive; antlers later shed naturally without any wound.
