Why this anatomy matters

Naked mole rats are effectively COLD-BLOODED — they don't maintain a constant body temperature. They have minimal hair, very low metabolism, and survive on the constant 30°C of their underground burrows. They are also resistant to cancer, can live without oxygen for 18 minutes, and live 30+ years. The closest mammal to behaving like a reptile.

Functional explanation

Most mammals are like household central heating — burn fuel constantly to keep warm. The naked mole rat is a passive house — uses the surrounding ground temperature for free heating.

Clinical relevance

Major research model — naked mole rats almost never get cancer. Understanding their high-molecular-weight hyaluronan + p53 pathway may reveal therapies for human cancer.
