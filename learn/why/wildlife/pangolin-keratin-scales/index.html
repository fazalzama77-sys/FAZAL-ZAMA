Why this anatomy matters

The pangolin is the ONLY mammal covered in true keratin scales (made of the same protein as hair, hooves, and nails — but arranged as overlapping plates like a reptile). When threatened, the pangolin rolls into a ball; its scales are razor-sharp at the edges. No domestic species has this protection.

Functional explanation

It's as if a dog or cat replaced all its hairs with a coat of overlapping fingernails. Same protein (keratin), totally different architecture.

Clinical relevance

Pangolin scales have no medicinal value despite traditional Chinese medicine claims — they're chemically identical to fingernails. Pangolins are critically endangered due to scale poaching + bushmeat trade.
