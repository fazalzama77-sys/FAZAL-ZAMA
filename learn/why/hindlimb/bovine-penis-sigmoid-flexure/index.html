Bull vs. Stallion

Why this anatomy matters

The bull, ram, and boar penis is FIBROELASTIC — short cavernous spaces, mostly dense collagen — held permanently flexed in an S-shape (the SIGMOID FLEXURE) behind the scrotum. Erection works by STRAIGHTENING the flexure (length increase only). The stallion penis is MUSCULOCAVERNOUS — huge cavernous spaces that engorge with blood (volume increase). Two completely different erection engineering.

Functional explanation

Bull penis = an extendable car aerial: stays the same thickness, just unfolds longer. Stallion penis = a balloon: gets thicker and longer by filling with blood.

Clinical relevance

Persistent frenulum (bull): Strap of tissue holds penis bent — cannot extend during mating. Paraphimosis (stallion): Engorged penis can't retract — emergency in horses, less issue in bull's fibroelastic penis which retracts mechanically via retractor penis muscle.
