Why this anatomy matters

The cow udder is held against the body wall by a unique combination of an elastic median suspensory ligament (paired, midline) + fibrous lateral suspensory ligaments. The elastic median ligament lets the udder expand massively as it fills with milk and recoil when emptied. Bitches and sows have multiple small mammae attached individually to the body wall — they don't need a single huge expandable bag.

Functional explanation

The udder is a giant water balloon. The lateral ligaments are the rigid handles holding it to the wall. The median ligament is the bungee cord at the centre — it stretches when the balloon fills and recoils when emptied. Cut the bungee, and the balloon sags between your knees.

Clinical relevance

Failure of the median suspensory ligament in old high-yielding cows produces a 'broken' or pendulous udder — teats angle outward, easily injured, prone to mastitis. Lifelong udder conformation depends on this ligament — a key trait in dairy cow selection.

Self-check question
