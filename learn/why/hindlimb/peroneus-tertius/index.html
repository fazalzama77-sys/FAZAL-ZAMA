Horse vs. Ox

Why this anatomy matters

In the Ox, this is a fleshy muscle flexing the hock. In the Horse, it exists as a tendinous cord (Reciprocal Apparatus). It mechanically links the stifle and hock: if the stifle flexes, the hock MUST flex.

Functional explanation

In the Ox, the ankle is moved by a motor. In the Horse, the ankle is connected to the knee by a solid steel rod. You cannot bend the knee without the rod pushing the ankle.

Clinical relevance

Rupture of the peroneus tertius in the horse is pathognomonic: the hock can be extended while the stifle is flexed, which is mechanically impossible in a healthy horse.

Self-check question

If a horse can extend its hock while the stifle is flexed, what has ruptured?
