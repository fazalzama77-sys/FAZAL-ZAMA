Horse vs. Ox

Equine Frog vs Bovine Digital Cushion: Veterinary Anatomy Explained | IVRI

Why this anatomy matters

The Horse hoof has a V-shaped rubbery 'FROG' on the sole — a wedge of soft horn that absorbs concussion + acts as a hydraulic pump pushing venous blood up the leg with every step. The Ox does NOT have a frog; instead each cloven claw has a soft 'DIGITAL CUSHION' deep inside the heel that does the shock absorption. Same job, totally different anatomy — horse keeps it external (sole-visible), ox keeps it internal.

Functional explanation

Horse foot = sneaker with the sole-cushion glued ON THE OUTSIDE (you can see it). Cow foot = sneaker with the cushion HIDDEN INSIDE the heel. Both cushion shock; only one is visible.

Clinical relevance

Thrush (horse): Bacterial infection of the frog → black smelly necrosis; treated by paring + topical antiseptic. Sole ulcer (cow): Damaged digital cushion under sole horn → exposed corium → lameness; classic dairy disease.

Self-check question

Which soft-tissue structure of the horse foot acts as a hydraulic pump for venous return?
