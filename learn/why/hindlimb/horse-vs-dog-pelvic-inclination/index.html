Horse vs. Dog

Why this anatomy matters

The horse pelvis is roughly HORIZONTAL — long axis of sacrum runs nearly parallel to the ground. The dog pelvis is INCLINED downward — sacrum + ilium tilted ~30° below horizontal. This affects gait: horses can extend the hindlimb backward through a long arc (galloping stride); dogs have a more compact pelvis for jumping/twisting agility.

Functional explanation

Horse pelvis is a long flat tray pointing backward. Dog pelvis is the same tray tipped down at the back like a slide.

Clinical relevance

Pelvimetry: Different acceptable dimensions for different species. Hip dysplasia (dog): The inclined pelvis allows incomplete acetabular coverage to be tolerated, but worsens with breed selection. Sacroiliac luxation: Different management approaches in horse (mostly conservative) vs dog (often surgical).
