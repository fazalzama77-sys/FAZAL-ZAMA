Why this anatomy matters

The PRE-PUBIC TENDON is a thick fibrous band attaching the rectus abdominis and pectineus muscles to the cranial edge of the pubis. It anchors the abdominal wall to the pelvis. In late-pregnant mares and cows, the weight of the gravid uterus + foetus + fluid can RUPTURE this tendon. Bitch pregnancies don't generate enough weight to threaten it.

Functional explanation

Imagine the abdominal wall as a hammock and the pre-pubic tendon as the rope holding one end. In a heavily pregnant mare, that rope can snap under load.

Clinical relevance

Pre-pubic Tendon Rupture (mare): Late-gestation emergency. The mare's belly suddenly drops; she can't push during foaling. Caesarean section + euthanasia decision often required. Pre-pubic tendon hernia: Partial rupture → ventral abdominal hernia.
