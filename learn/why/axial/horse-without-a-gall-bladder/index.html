Horse vs. Ox/Dog

Why this anatomy matters

The Horse is the only common domestic animal without a gall bladder — bile flows continuously from the liver into the duodenum. The Ox, Dog, and Pig all have gall bladders to store and concentrate bile for release at meals. The horse is designed as a continuous grazer; with food in the gut almost all the time, there is no need to store bile for episodic meals.

Functional explanation

The dog has a kitchen pantry (gall bladder) for bile — stored and released at mealtime. The horse is a 24-hour buffet — bile drips constantly straight into the gut, so the pantry was never built.

Clinical relevance

You cannot perform 'gall bladder biopsy' or cholecystectomy on a horse — there is no organ to remove. In contrast, gall bladder disease is common in dogs (mucocele) and cattle (fasciolosis-related cholecystitis).
