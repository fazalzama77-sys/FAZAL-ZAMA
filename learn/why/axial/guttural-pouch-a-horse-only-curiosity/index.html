Why this anatomy matters

The Horse uniquely has paired guttural pouches — large air-filled diverticula of the auditory (Eustachian) tube, ~300 mL each, lying ventral to the skull. The Ox, Dog, and other domestic species have only a small auditory tube without a pouch. Function is debated — possibly cooling of carotid arterial blood that runs through the pouch wall, or middle-ear pressure equalization during galloping head movement.

Functional explanation

Imagine attaching a small balloon to the side of your ear-canal tube — that's the guttural pouch. The horse, the ear-tube has expanded into a sinus-like cavity that quietly hosts the carotid artery. When fungus moves in, it can chew through the artery wall.

Clinical relevance

Guttural Pouch Mycosis (Aspergillus): fungus erodes the internal carotid artery in the pouch wall → fatal epistaxis. Guttural Pouch Empyema: pus accumulation, surgical drainage via Viborg's triangle. These conditions exist ONLY in horses.
