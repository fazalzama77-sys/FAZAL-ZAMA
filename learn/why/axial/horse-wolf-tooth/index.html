Why this anatomy matters

Many horses have a small vestigial 1st premolar (PM1) called the WOLF TOOTH sitting just in front of the cheek-teeth. It serves no chewing function but interferes with the bit. NO other domestic species has this — it is a non-functional remnant of the original 4-premolar Equidae dentition pattern.

Functional explanation

Like a vestigial appendix — non-functional but occasionally needs surgical removal.

Clinical relevance

Wolf tooth extraction is one of the most common equine dental procedures, done before bitting young horses. Failure to remove → headshaking, bit resistance, training problems. Some horses also have 'blind wolf teeth' (unerupted), diagnosed on radiograph.
