Why this anatomy matters

Sows have multiple paired mammary glands (6-8 pairs) arranged in a row from thorax to inguinal region — adapted for litter pregnancies. Each teat has 2 streak canals. Cow has 4 quarters with 4 teats — adapted for one (occasionally two) calves. Bitch similar to sow (5 pairs); mare has 2 teats only (single foal).

Functional explanation

Cow udder = single 4-tap kitchen tank. Sow udder = a long row of 16 mini-taps along the belly, one per piglet.

Clinical relevance

'Teat order' in piglets: Newborn piglets establish a hierarchy by claiming a specific teat — best teats (cranial = more milk) go to dominant piglets. Failure to suckle from a personal teat → starvation. Bovine mastitis: Easier to manage per-quarter due to functional independence.

Self-check question

How many streak canals open at each teat of a sow?
