Why this anatomy matters

The FRONTAL SINUS in the ox is enormous — it extends from the forehead back into the horn cores and laterally over the orbit. The horse frontal sinus is also large but doesn't enter the horn (no horn). The dog frontal sinus is small. Bovine sinus design accommodates the horn anchorage + reduces skull weight.

Functional explanation

Cow skull is like an inflated balloon at the top — much of it is air-filled space rather than solid bone. Reduces head weight AND provides horn anchorage.

Clinical relevance

Sinusitis (horse): Common; pus accumulates → unilateral nasal discharge + facial swelling. Sinusitis (ox): Often follows dehorning if horn base infection spreads to sinus (the cavities communicate). Trephination (drilling a hole through the skull to drain sinus pus) is a routine procedure with anatomy-specific entry points.
