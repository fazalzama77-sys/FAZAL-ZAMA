Why this anatomy matters

The rumen, reticulum, and omasum of cattle are lined by STRATIFIED SQUAMOUS KERATINIZED epithelium — the same tough tissue as your skin. The horse and dog stomach have only GLANDULAR epithelium throughout. Cattle have this skin-like lining so the rough, abrasive forage and microbial environment doesn't shred the stomach wall.

Functional explanation

Cattle's rumen is leather-lined like a workman's glove — designed to handle rough material. Horse stomach is silk-lined — meant for soft chewed paste only.

Clinical relevance

Parakeratosis (cow): Excessive keratinisation of rumen papillae from high-grain diets → reduced VFA absorption, poor weight gain. Equine gastric ulcers (EGUS): Horse has a small non-glandular region only at the cardia (margo plicatus) — ulcers form there from acid splash during exercise.

Self-check question

Which compartment of the bovine stomach is lined by GLANDULAR (NOT keratinised) epithelium?
