Cattle vs. Deer (antlers)

Why this anatomy matters

Cattle HORNS are permanent bony cores (extensions of the frontal bone) covered with a keratin sheath. They grow throughout life, are never shed, and are HOLLOW INSIDE (continuous with the frontal sinus in adults). Deer ANTLERS are completely different: solid bone, deciduous (shed annually), and not covered with keratin — covered with velvet skin during growth.

Functional explanation

Cow horn = a fingernail growing on a finger of bone, never shed. Deer antler = a tree branch grown fresh every year and dropped in winter.

Clinical relevance

Dehorning (cattle): Painful procedure; nerve block of cornual branch of zygomaticotemporal nerve required. In mature cattle, sawing through horn opens the frontal sinus → sinusitis risk. Disbudding (calves): Cautery destruction of horn bud before bony horn develops — quicker, less painful.
