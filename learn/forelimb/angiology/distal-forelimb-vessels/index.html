Veterinary anatomy overview

Cattle: The median artery supplies palmar common digital arteries and then axial and abaxial proper digital arteries for digits III and IV. Dorsal and palmar networks communicate around the carpus, metacarpus and digits. Veins form paired deep channels and a rich superficial/digital network.

Detailed anatomy

DISTAL VASCULAR PATTERN

At the bovine carpus and metacarpus, median, radial and interosseous contributions form dorsal and palmar anastomoses. Palmar common digital vessels divide into proper digital vessels along axial and abaxial surfaces of the two principal digits. Digital arteries supply the corium, distal phalanx and associated soft tissues. Because bovine and equine distal nomenclature differs, equine medial/lateral palmar-artery descriptions must not be copied unchanged to the cloven foot.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Paired functional digits receive axial and abaxial proper digital vessels.

Horse

Medial and lateral palmar digital arteries supply the single digit.

Dog

Dorsal and palmar metacarpal vessels supply multiple digits.

Clinical relevance

Clinical relevance: Distal intravenous regional anesthesia uses a suitable vein under a tourniquet; it is not an intra-arterial technique.
