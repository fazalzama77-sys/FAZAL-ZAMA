Veterinary anatomy overview

Sequence (Ox):
Subclavian → Axillary → Brachial → Median → Palmar Common Digital II/III/IV → Proper Palmar Digital arteries.

• Axillary a. begins at first rib, lies in axillary space — chief vessel of the forelimb.
• Brachial a. = continuation along medial arm; gives off Deep Brachial → musculospiral groove.
• Median a. is the main continuation in the forearm (NOT radial like in humans!) — runs through carpal canal.
• Distally splits into common digital arteries that supply the cloven hoof.

Cephalic vein on cranial forearm = standard IV site in dog.
Jugular vein = main IV site in cattle/horse (not a forelimb vein, but commonly grouped clinically).

Detailed anatomy

FORELIMB ARTERIAL TREE

In cattle the aortic arch gives a single brachiocephalic trunk. That trunk gives rise to both subclavian arteries and the bicarotid trunk; the left subclavian does not arise directly from the arch. Each subclavian continues as axillary, brachial and chiefly median arterial pathways to the palmar common and proper digital arteries. The radial artery is comparatively small but should not be described as universally absent or functionless.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Median artery is dominant distally; the radial artery is comparatively small but present. Palmar common and proper digital arteries supply the claws.

Horse

Median artery is also dominant. Pulse routinely taken at facial artery at mandible (not forelimb). Distal limb: medial palmar digital artery used for diagnostic 'palmar digital block' pulse check.

Dog

Cephalic vein is the routine IV site. Brachial pulse felt on medial elbow. Femoral pulse (hindlimb) commonly used for cardiac assessment.

Fowl

Brachial artery main vessel. Wing veins (basilic / ulnar) used for venepuncture in birds (radiometacarpal anastomosis).

Clinical relevance

Cephalic Venepuncture (Dog): Distal cranial forearm — most common IV site in small animal practice.
Jugular vein in large animals — a neck-region vessel and the principal large-animal IV site.
Hoof Pulse Examination (Horse): Increased digital pulse at the abaxial sesamoid → laminitis warning.
Aneurysm/Thrombosis of Brachial/Axillary Artery: Compression neuropathy of the median + musculocutaneous nerves due to Ansa Axillaris relationship.
