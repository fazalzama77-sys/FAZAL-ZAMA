Veterinary anatomy overview

The bovine fetlock comprises paired metacarpophalangeal articulations for digits III and IV with four proximal sesamoids. The interosseous/suspensory structure, extensor branches, distal sesamoidean ligaments and interdigital ligaments support the joint. Exact distal sesamoidean ligament pattern and nomenclature are species-specific and should not be reduced to a universal claim that the straight ligament is absent.

Detailed anatomy

BOVINE FETLOCK SUPPORT

Each functional digit articulates with its metacarpal trochlea and paired proximal sesamoids. Interosseous branches attach to the sesamoids and send extensor branches dorsally. Distal sesamoidean and interdigital ligaments limit excessive extension and splaying. The bovine interosseous retains variable muscle fibers; the equine suspensory may also retain residual fibers. Relationships and ligament names should follow species-specific references rather than unsupported absence claims.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Two functional fetlock articulations with interdigital stabilization and a suspensory structure retaining variable muscle fibers.

Horse

One principal fetlock; suspensory ligament and distal sesamoidean ligaments are highly developed and may contain residual muscle fibers.

Dog

Separate metacarpophalangeal joints with muscular interossei.

Clinical relevance

Septic Arthritis: Common sequel to penetrating wounds (nails/wire) or foot rot extension; requires palmar pouch lavage.

Suspensory Breakdown: Desmitis causes 'sinking' fetlock (hyperextension) beyond normal limits, altering hoof flight and causing sole ulcers.

Interdigital Hyperplasia: Laxity of interdigital ligaments from poorly conformed heavy cattle leads to splaying, skin irritation, and proliferative granulomas (corns).
