Veterinary anatomy overview

A composite synovial joint with antebrachiocarpal, middle carpal and carpometacarpal levels. Most flexion occurs proximally, while distal motion is more limited. Synovial communications vary by species and individual; the middle carpal and carpometacarpal compartments commonly communicate. Do not assign fixed universal flexion angles or assume complete sac isolation.

Detailed anatomy

CARPAL JOINT

The antebrachiocarpal, middle carpal and carpometacarpal levels form a composite joint. Motion and synovial communications vary by species; the middle carpal and carpometacarpal compartments commonly communicate. Fixed normal angles and universal lavage-port rules are inappropriate. Septic-joint lavage must target the affected compartment using current clinical anatomy.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Seven to eight carpal bones (C1 rudimentary/variable); C3 massive load-bearing; similar three-level architecture but C1 present.

Dog

Radial and intermediate fused into 'intermedioradial'; seven bones total; thinner palmar ligament.

Clinical relevance

Clinical relevance: Precarpal hygroma is extra-articular. Septic arthritis requires identification of the involved synovial compartment and lavage planned from current species-specific anatomy; a universal two-needle technique cannot be prescribed from this overview.
