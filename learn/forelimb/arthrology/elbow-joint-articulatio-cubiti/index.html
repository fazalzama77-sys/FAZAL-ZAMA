Veterinary anatomy overview

Synovial compound hinge (ginglymus) functioning as a stable compound hinge; it has no special snap-lock stay mechanism.

Articular surfaces: Humeral trochlea (medial and lateral condyles separated by synovial groove) articulates with radial head (fovea capitis) and ulnar trochlear notch with anconeal process.

The lateral and medial collateral ligaments stabilize the joint but do not create a bovine elbow snap-lock mechanism.

Medial collateral ligament has long and short parts inserting on medial radial tuberosity and coronoid process.

Radius and ulna fused via synostosis in adults (radioulnar ligaments ossified), eliminating pronation/supination. Proximal and distal interosseous spaces remain and transmit species-specific vessels.

Anconeal process seats in the olecranon fossa during extension, contributing ordinary bony congruity.

Clinical: Capped elbow (olecranon bursitis) from trauma; fracture prognosis depends on location, displacement, soft-tissue injury and patient factors.

Detailed anatomy

ELBOW JOINT

The elbow combines humeroradial and humeroulnar articulations in a common functional hinge. Collateral ligaments and congruent articular surfaces restrict motion mainly to flexion and extension. Cattle have species-specific radioulnar fusion and interosseous spaces, but no established anconeal or collateral "snap-lock" mechanism comparable to the equine patellar lock.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

No special elbow snap-lock mechanism but radioulnar fusion less complete distally; olecranon bursitis ('capped elbow') equally common.

Dog

Separate radius and ulna allowing pronation/supination; true snap mechanism absent; elbow dysplasia common.

Clinical relevance

Capped Elbow (Bursitis): Subcutaneous olecranon bursa inflammation from lying on concrete ('shoe boil'); extra-articular swelling distinct from joint effusion.

Fracture Patterns: Humeral spiral fractures common due to radioulnar fusion preventing interosseous give.

Radial Nerve Paralysis: Combined with triceps failure eliminates ability to lock elbow, causing 'dropped elbow' inability to bear weight.
