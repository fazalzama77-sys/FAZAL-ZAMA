Veterinary anatomy overview

Synovial ginglymus between middle (P2) and distal (P3/coffin) phalanges including navicular bone.

Articular surfaces: Distal P2 articulates with proximal P3; navicular bone (distal sesamoid) lies palmar to joint surface.

Navicular bone suspended by collateral sesamoidean ligaments from P1 and anchored distally by impar ligament to flexor surface of P3.

Deep digital flexor tendon (DDFT) passes over navicular bone enclosed within navicular bursa reducing friction.

Collateral ligaments connect P2 to P3 bilaterally.

Vulnerable to 'street nail' injuries penetrating sole and entering navicular bursa causing septic bursitis—catastrophic lameness requiring surgical drainage.

Detailed anatomy

DISTAL INTERPHALANGEAL JOINT

The DIP joint is formed by P2, P3 and the distal sesamoid bone. The deep digital flexor tendon glides over the flexor surface of the distal sesamoid, with the navicular bursa between tendon and bone. The bursa and DIP joint are normally separate; an acquired communication may indicate injury. Penetrating foot wounds require imaging and specialist treatment rather than a universal surgical approach.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Navicular bone larger with distinct navicular syndrome (bone degeneration); single digit makes salvage critical.

Dog

P3 called 'ungual process' with ungual crest; navicular smaller; joints more mobile.

Clinical relevance

Septic Navicular Bursitis: Catastrophic lameness from penetrating foreign body; requires immediate surgical drainage via street nail procedure.

Coffin Joint Septic Arthritis: Tracks from sole abscesses or white line disease; difficult to treat due to enclosed anatomy.

Impar Ligament Injury: Rare but contributes to navicular bone instability and chronic foot lameness.
