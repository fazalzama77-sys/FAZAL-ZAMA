Veterinary anatomy overview

Type: Intrinsic, Forearm (Extensor Group by nerve, Flexor by action)

Origin: Lateral epicondyle of humerus

Insertion: Accessory carpal bone and proximal rudimentary fifth metacarpal (Mc V)

Function: Flexes carpus (despite radial innervation); abducts limb minimally.

Detailed anatomy

THE CARPAL FLEXOR PARADOX

Morphologically classified as an extensor (supplied by radial nerve) but positioned caudal to the axis of rotation, making it functionally a carpal flexor.

ACCESS CARPAL ANCHOR: Inserts on the prominent accessory carpal bone, the pulley-like bone on the caudolateral carpus. This insertion provides mechanical advantage for flexion and lateral stability.

rudimentary DIGIT CONNECTION: Also inserts on the rudimentary fifth metacarpal; this is not the entire dewclaw, representing the structural remnant of digit V.

LATERAL STABILITY: Provides lateral support to the carpus during weight bearing, preventing excessive medial deviation of the carpus.

NEURAL BLOCK LANDMARK: The groove between Ulnaris Lateralis and Flexor Carpi Ulnaris is the critical landmark for performing the Ulnar Nerve Block at the distal forearm level.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Similar paradoxical flexor function; same landmark usage.

Dog

Separate tendon slips to digit V when present.

Clinical relevance

Ulnar Nerve Block Site: Palpable groove at a species-appropriate level proximal to the accessory carpal bone between this muscle and FCU is THE landmark for ulnar block. Carpal Flexion Weakness: Rarely injured alone but contributes to carpal drop in severe flexor deficits.
