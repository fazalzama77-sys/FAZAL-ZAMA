Veterinary anatomy overview

Type: Intrinsic, Brachial (Accessory)

Origin: Caudal border of scapula and latissimus dorsi fascia

Insertion: Olecranon and antebrachial fascia

Function: Extends elbow; tenses antebrachial fascia.

Detailed anatomy

TENSOR FASCIAE ANTEBRACHII

This thin muscle arises from the latissimus-dorsi tendon and caudal scapular region and inserts on the olecranon and antebrachial fascia. It assists extension of the elbow and tenses the antebrachial fascia. Do not assign it an unproven hydrostatic compartment-pressure mechanism.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Similar origin and function; clinically significant for fascial closure.

Dog

Less developed; less fascial integration.

Clinical relevance

Clinical relevance: Injury may weaken elbow extension together with triceps injury. Compartment syndrome should be described from documented vascular and fascial pathology, not attributed specifically to this muscle.
