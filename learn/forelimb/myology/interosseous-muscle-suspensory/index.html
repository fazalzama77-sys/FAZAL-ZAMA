Veterinary anatomy overview

Type: Intrinsic, Manus

Origin: Proximal metacarpus and distal carpal bones

Insertion: Proximal sesamoid bones (4 total—2 per digit) and extensor tendons (via extensor branches)

Function: Suspensory apparatus preventing fetlock hyperextension; supports fetlock during stance.

Detailed anatomy

INTEROSSEOUS MUSCLE / SUSPENSORY LIGAMENT

The interosseous structure supports the fetlock and gives extensor branches distally. It retains variable muscle fibers: generally more in cattle and fewer in the adult horse, but the equine suspensory is not universally devoid of muscle. It is distinct from the accessory (check) ligaments of the digital flexor tendons. Claims that bovine muscle content causes cattle to fatigue on hard surfaces are unsupported.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Retains more muscle fibers on average and functions chiefly as a suspensory structure.

Horse

Adult suspensory ligament usually contains fewer but variable residual muscle fibers; it is not universally entirely tendinous.

Dog

Interosseous muscles remain muscular and contribute to digital movement.

Clinical relevance

Clinical relevance: Desmitis or rupture weakens fetlock support. Diagnosis and prognosis depend on species, lesion site and imaging; do not infer fatigue or lesion severity from muscle-fiber content alone.
