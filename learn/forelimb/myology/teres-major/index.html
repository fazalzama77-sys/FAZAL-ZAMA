Veterinary anatomy overview

Type: Intrinsic, Scapulohumeral (Medial)

Origin: Caudal angle and caudal border of scapula

Insertion: Teres major tuberosity on medial humerus (common with latissimus dorsi)

Function: Flexes shoulder joint and adducts limb.

Detailed anatomy

THE CAUDAL MEDIAL FLEXOR

Located on the medial aspect just caudal to the shoulder joint, forming a functional unit with the latissimus dorsi (shared insertion).

ORIGIN SITES: Arises from the caudal angle (distal tip) and caudal border of the scapula, giving it a caudal line of pull compared to other shoulder flexors.

SYNERGISTIC INSERTION: Inserts via common tendon with latissimus dorsi on the teres major tuberosity. This combined insertion creates one of the most powerful retractor/adductor complexes in the body.

QUADRANGULAR SPACE: Forms the caudal boundary of the quadrangular space through which the axillary nerve passes. Surgical approaches to the axillary nerve or shoulder must account for teres major position.

FUNCTION: Flexes the shoulder (drawing limb forward/upward) and adducts the limb toward the body wall.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Similar position; also shares tendon with latissimus.

Dog

More distinct separation from latissimus insertion.

Clinical relevance

Caudal Dislocation: Helps resist caudal displacement of humeral head; injury allows caudal 'slipping'.
