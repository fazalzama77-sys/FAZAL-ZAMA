Veterinary anatomy overview

Type: Fascial/Muscular remnants

Components: Lumbricals, Interdigital muscles

Function: Adduction of claws (prevent splaying); minor proprioceptive roles.

Detailed anatomy

DISTAL RETINACULAR AND INTERDIGITAL STRUCTURES

Palmar and digital annular ligaments retain the flexor tendons against the digit and prevent bowstringing. Proximal and distal interdigital ligaments limit excessive separation of bovine digits. Lumbrical and small intrinsic muscles vary by species and should not be presented as the principal mechanism controlling independent claw placement. Annular ligaments are fibrous retainers, not muscular remnants.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

No interdigital muscles (single digit); different annular system.

Dog

Multiple interossei and lumbricals corresponding to 4-5 digits.

Clinical relevance

Interdigital Fibroma: Hyperplasia of interdigital skin into a 'corn.' Tendon Displacement: Annular ligament rupture causes bowstringing of flexor tendons. Claw Integrity: Loss of adduction function leads to splayed toes and hoof wear abnormalities.
