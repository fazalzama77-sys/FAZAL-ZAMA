Veterinary anatomy overview

Type: Intrinsic, Forearm (Caudomedial/Flexor Group)

Origin: Medial epicondyle of humerus

Insertion: Palmar aspect of proximal metacarpus (Mc III)

Function: Flexes the carpus (primary flexor).

Detailed anatomy

THE CARPAL FLEXOR LEADER

The most cranial of the caudal (flexor) group muscles, lying directly medial on the forearm deep to the pectoral retinacula.

STRAIGHT LINE PULL: Originates from the medial epicondyle and runs straight down the medial forearm to insert on the palmar aspect of the proximal cannon bone (Mc III). This direct line provides maximum mechanical advantage for carpal flexion.

CARPAL CANAL RELATIONSHIP: Passes over the palmar carpus but outside the carpal canal proper (which contains the digital flexors). Covered by the flexor retinaculum at the carpus.

FUNCTIONAL DUALITY: While primarily a flexor, it provides active check against hyperextension during the weight-bearing phase, working in concert with the passive stay apparatus.

MEDIAN NERVE RELATIONSHIP: The median nerve runs deep to this muscle in the proximal forearm; the muscle serves as a landmark for median nerve blocks (inject just cranial to FCR tendon at carpus).

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Similar position and function; larger for support.

Dog

Splits into superficial and deep parts in some breeds.

Clinical relevance

Clinical relevance: The tendon is a palpable medial landmark at the distal forearm. Regional anesthesia must use current palmar terminology and species-specific landmarks; this muscle alone is not a universal landmark for a high palmar block.
