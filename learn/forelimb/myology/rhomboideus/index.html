Veterinary anatomy overview

Type: Extrinsic, Deep Layer

Origin: Funicular nuchal ligament [Cervicis]; Spinous processes T2-T7 [Thoracis]

Insertion: Medial surface of dorsal scapular cartilage

Function: Elevates scapula and draws it medially toward trunk; stabilizes dorsal apex during stance phase.

Detailed anatomy

THE SCAPULAR ANCHOR

Lying deep to the trapezius, the rhomboideus provides the medial stabilizing force for the scapular girdle, anchoring the dorsal border to the axial skeleton.

SUBDIVISIONS:
• Rhomboideus Cervicis: Arises from the funicular nuchal ligament (larmier)
• Rhomboideus Thoracis: Arises from spinous processes of cranial thoracic vertebrae (T2-T7)
• Rhomboideus Capitis: ABSENT or rudimentary in ox (critical distinction from carnivores where it reaches the occiput)

INSERTION SPECIFICS: Muscle inserts on the medial surface of the dorsal scapular cartilage (cartilago scapulae), amplifying the surface area for thoracic attachment and shock dissipation.

BIOMECHANICAL ROLE: Presses the cartilage against the trunk, preventing lateral deviation of the scapula during the stance phase. This medial pull is vital for maintaining the rigid column needed for weight support, counteracting the lateral abduction forces generated during weight bearing.

Comparative anatomy

Dog

Possesses prominent rhomboideus capitis attaching to occiput; absent in ox.

Horse

Similar division but attaches more extensively to cervical vertebrae.

Clinical relevance

Atrophy Indicators: Wasting creates instability at the shoulder dorsal angle, contributing to lateral 'popping' in conjunction with sweeney.
