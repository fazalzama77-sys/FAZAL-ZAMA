Veterinary anatomy overview

Type: Intrinsic craniolateral antebrachial muscle

Origin: Lateral surface of the radius

Insertion: Medial proximal metacarpal region, varying by species

Function: Assists extension and medial stabilization of the carpus. Equine splint-bone terminology must not be applied to cattle.

Detailed anatomy

EXTENSOR CARPI OBLIQUUS / ABDUCTOR DIGITI I LONGUS

This small oblique muscle crosses the dorsal carpus from the radius to the medial proximal metacarpal region. Its insertion and nomenclature vary by species. In cattle it should not be described as inserting on an equine medial splint bone.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Similar; often called Abductor Digiti I Longus.

Dog

True abductor of digit II when present.

Clinical relevance

Clinical relevance: Its tendon is encountered in dorsal carpal approaches. Tendon-sheath relations are species-specific and should not be stated to be universally shared with extensor carpi radialis.
