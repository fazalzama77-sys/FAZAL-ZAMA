Veterinary anatomy overview

Type: Extrinsic, Deep Layer

Subtypes: Descending (manubrium), Transverse (sternal), Deep/Ascending (sternum/xiphoid)

Insertion: Greater/lesser tubercles of humerus; deltoid crest

Function: Adduct limb; prevent abduction during weight bearing; assist in advancing trunk (deep part) or limb (superficial).

Detailed anatomy

PECTORAL MUSCLES

The superficial pectorals adduct the limb and attach from the sternum to the humerus and antebrachial fascia in species-specific patterns. The deep pectoral arises from the ventral sternum and adjacent fascia and inserts on the humerus and shoulder fascia according to species. Together they stabilize the limb against abduction; the deep pectoral retracts the free limb and can advance the trunk when the limb is fixed. Exact tubercular attachments must not be generalized across cattle, horse and dog.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Deep pectoral (ascending) is massive; subclavius is distinct and robust.

Dog

Deep pectoral has distinct abdominal attachment; accessory head common.

Clinical relevance

Deep Pectoral Tear: Rare but causes limb abduction inability, 'sprawling' gait. Mastitis Extension: Infections can track along pectoral fascia to forelimb lymphatics.
