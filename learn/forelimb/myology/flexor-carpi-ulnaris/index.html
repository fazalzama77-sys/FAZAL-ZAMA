Veterinary anatomy overview

Type: Intrinsic, Forearm (Caudomedial/Ulnar Group)

Heads: Humeral head (medial epicondyle) & Ulnar head (olecranon)

Insertion: Accessory carpal bone

Function: Flexes carpus (ulnar aspect).

Detailed anatomy

THE ULNAR CARPAL FLEXOR

Located caudal/medial in the forearm, providing ulnar-sided flexion and stabilization.

DUAL-HEADED ORIGIN: Unique among flexors in having two origins:
• Humeral Head: Arises from medial epicondyle (with other flexors)
• Ulnar Head: Arises from medial surface of the olecranon (ulna), giving it origin from the forearm itself

ACCESSORY CARPAL INSERTION: Inserts on the prominent accessory carpal bone on the caudolateral carpus—the same bone receiving the Ulnaris Lateralis but from the flexor side.

ULNAR STABILITY: Provides the flexor counterbalance to the Ulnaris Lateralis, creating a force couple stabilizing the carpus in the frontal plane during weight bearing. Prevents excessive abduction/adduction of the carpus.

ULNAR NERVE BLOCK LANDMARK: Forms the medial border of the groove (with Ulnaris Lateralis forming the lateral border) used for ulnar nerve blocks.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Similar dual heads; ulnar head larger in horse.

Dog

Ulnar head may be absent or fused in some individuals.

Clinical relevance

Ulnar Nerve Block Landmark: Palpate groove between FCU and ulnaris lateralis at a species-appropriate level proximal to the accessory carpal bone. Carpal Valgus/Varus: Asymmetry between left/right FCU tone contributes to angular limb deformities.
