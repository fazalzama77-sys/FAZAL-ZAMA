Veterinary anatomy overview

Bovine manus: Median and ulnar nerves contribute palmar branches; the superficial radial nerve contributes dorsal branches. Palmar and dorsal common digital nerves divide into axial and abaxial proper digital nerves for digits III and IV. Territories overlap around the claws.

Detailed anatomy

DISTAL FORELIMB INNERVATION

At the carpus and metacarpus, median, ulnar and superficial-radial branches form communicating dorsal and palmar pathways. Each functional digit receives axial and abaxial proper-digital branches. The deep ulnar branch supplies interosseous structures. Equine palmar/palmar-metacarpal terminology and block levels differ from bovine common/proper-digital anatomy, so blocks must be named by exact nerve and level.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Two functional digits have axial and abaxial palmar/dorsal proper digital nerves.

Horse

Medial/lateral palmar nerves and palmar metacarpal nerves serve the single digit at defined levels.

Dog

Palmar and dorsal digital branches serve multiple digits with overlapping territories.

Clinical relevance

Blocks: Palmar, palmar-metacarpal and digital techniques are not interchangeable across cattle and horses; use current species-specific landmarks and calculated dosing.
