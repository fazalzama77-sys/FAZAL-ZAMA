Veterinary anatomy overview

Formation: Ventral rami of C6, C7, C8, T1, and T2 (Post-fixed arrangement).

Key Features:
- Located deep in axillary space
- Forms broad 'plexus sheet' around axillary artery
- C6-C7: Cranial roots (shoulder/stabilization)
- C8-T2: Caudal roots (flexors/foot)

Clinical Significance:
T2 inclusion serves massive pectoral muscles and cutaneous trunci (fly swatting reflex).

Detailed anatomy

BRACHIAL PLEXUS

In cattle the plexus is formed chiefly by ventral branches C6-T2, with variation. The canine plexus is also classically C6-T2, so T2 is not a uniquely ruminant contribution. Roots intermingle to form the named limb nerves; clinical localization should use motor, sensory and reflex findings rather than assigning one function rigidly to a single root.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Chiefly C6-T2, with individual variation.

Horse

Chiefly C6-T2; segmental contributions vary.

Dog

Classically C6-T2, not C5-T1.

Clinical relevance

Root Avulsion: Traction injuries can avulse C6-T1 roots from spinal cord. Axillary Trauma: Deep penetration wounds or hematomas can compress entire plexus causing complete forelimb paresis.
