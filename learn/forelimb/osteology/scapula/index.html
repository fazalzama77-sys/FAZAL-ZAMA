Veterinary anatomy overview

Triangular flat bone (os planum).

Ox Standard:
- Spine is sinuous and divides lateral surface into 1:3 ratio (Supra:Infra).
- Acromion process is pointed and distinct.
- Subscapular fossa is shallow.
- Glenoid cavity is circular without a notch.

Detailed anatomy

SCAPULA — THE SHOULDER BLADE

The scapula is a flat triangular bone (os planum) that forms the proximal part of the forelimb girdle. In domestic mammals it has NO bony attachment to the axial skeleton — it is suspended from the trunk by a muscular sling ('synsarcosis'), which lets it glide cranio-caudally to add length to the stride.

BORDERS (3):
• Cranial border — slightly convex; gives origin to supraspinatus.
• Caudal border — thicker; gives origin to teres major and the long head of triceps.
• Dorsal border — bears the scapular cartilage (cartilago scapulae) in large herbivores — increases the available muscular attachment area.

ANGLES (3):
• Cranial angle — between cranial and dorsal borders.
• Caudal angle — between caudal and dorsal borders.
• Ventral angle (glenoid angle) — distal; bears the glenoid cavity.

SURFACES (2):
• Lateral surface — divided by the scapular spine into:
– Supraspinous fossa (cranial — narrow in ox, ratio 1:3) for supraspinatus.
– Infraspinous fossa (caudal — wide) for infraspinatus.
• Medial (Costal) surface — has the subscapular fossa for subscapularis (shallow in ox; deep in horse).

KEY FEATURES:
• Spine of scapula — vertical ridge on lateral surface; sinuous (S-shaped) in ox; ends distally as the ACROMION in ox & dog (ABSENT in horse).
• Tuber spinae — thickening of the middle of the spine; very prominent in the horse; the horse lacks an acromion.
• Glenoid cavity — shallow oval socket at ventral angle; receives the head of humerus → shoulder joint.
• Supraglenoid tubercle — small projection cranial to glenoid; origin of biceps brachii.
• Coracoid process — small medial projection of supraglenoid tubercle; origin of coracobrachialis.
• Suprascapular nerve relation — the nerve turns around the cranial border/neck of the scapula, where it is vulnerable to trauma; it does not cross a notch at the base of the spine.

UG MEMORY HOOK: 'Triangular bone, 3 borders, 3 angles, 2 surfaces, 1 spine ending in acromion (in ox/dog) or tuber spinae (in horse).'

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Acromion ABSENT. Spine fades distally. Prominent 'Tuber Spinae' present. Glenoid notch present.

Dog

Acromion prominent with 'Hamate Process'. Spine high, 1:1 fossa ratio.

Pig

Tuber spinae is very prominent and recurved caudally.

Fowl

Saber-like blade. No spine/fossae. Parallel to vertebrae.

Clinical relevance

Sweeney Shoulder (Horse): Atrophy of supra/infraspinatus muscles due to suprascapular nerve damage as it crosses the neck of the scapula.
