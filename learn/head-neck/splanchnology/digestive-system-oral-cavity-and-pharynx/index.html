Veterinary anatomy overview

Overview:
• The oral cavity is the portal of the digestive tube.
• In ruminants, the dental pad replaces upper incisors — adapted for grasping roughage.
• The Pharynx is a muscular tube common to both respiratory and digestive pathways.
• Key features: Tongue (torus linguae in Ox), Teeth, Salivary Glands, and Soft Palate.

Detailed anatomy

Oral Cavity and Pharyngeal Architecture

The splanchnology of the head begins with the oral cavity (cavum oris), which in ruminants is specialized for prehension and mastication of fibrous feed. Upper incisors and the upper canine are absent; a cornified dental pad occupies their region.

The bovine tongue bears a distinctive torus linguae (lingual torus) — a firm dorsal swelling of smooth muscle and connective tissue in the middle third of the tongue that aids in grasping forage.

The pharynx acts as a critical switching station where the respiratory and digestive tracts cross. It is divided into the oropharynx, nasopharynx, and laryngopharynx. The epiglottis acts as the switching valve during deglutition.

The salivary glands of ruminants are enormous — the parotid alone can weigh 400 g — because ruminants require vast quantities of alkaline saliva (up to 150 L/day in cattle) to buffer the fermentation acids in the rumen.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Dental pad (pulvinus dentalis) occupies the upper incisor and canine region. Large torus linguae. Parotid gland is extensive. Soft palate is relatively short.

Horse

Has upper incisors. The long soft palate makes the horse an obligate nasal breather. Vomiting is extremely difficult because of the powerful cardia/lower oesophageal sphincter, acute angle of oesophageal entry and associated stomach anatomy.

Dog

Has both upper and lower incisors. Soft palate is short. Vomiting reflex is well developed.

Clinical relevance

Pharyngeal Paralysis: Can lead to dysphagia and aspiration pneumonia. Actinobacillosis (Wooden Tongue): Actinobacillus lignieresii causes granulomatous glossitis in cattle — tongue becomes hard and swollen. Calf Diphtheria: Fusobacterium necrophorum causes necrotic stomatitis/pharyngitis in calves.
