Veterinary anatomy overview

Carotid sheath: Deep cervical fascia encloses the common carotid artery, vagosympathetic trunk and related lymphatic/neural structures; the internal jugular vein is small and variably associated in large domestic animals. The recurrent laryngeal nerve runs in the tracheoesophageal region rather than inside the superficial jugular furrow.

Detailed anatomy

DEEP NECK NEUROVASCULAR BUNDLE

The common carotid and vagosympathetic trunk ascend deep to the jugular furrow within a fascial compartment beside the trachea and esophagus. The sympathetic trunk separates from the vagus near the cranial cervical ganglion. The cervical trunk, recurrent laryngeal nerve and deep lymphatics have distinct relations. This topography explains why a poorly placed deep jugular injection can injure an artery or nerve even though these structures are not normal superficial furrow contents.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Common carotid and vagosympathetic trunk are deep; the external jugular remains superficial in the furrow.

Horse

Carotid-jugular relationships change from cranial to caudal neck and matter during venipuncture.

Dog

Internal and external jugular pathways and sheath relations differ from large ungulates.

Clinical relevance

Application: Jugular access and neck surgery require awareness of depth, side and level; use species-specific landmarks.
