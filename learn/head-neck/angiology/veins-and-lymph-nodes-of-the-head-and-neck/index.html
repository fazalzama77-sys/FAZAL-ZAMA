Veterinary anatomy overview

Major Veins:
• External Jugular Vein — chief drainage of head; in jugular furrow; standard IV site in cattle/horse.
Formed by union of linguofacial + maxillary veins at the angle of mandible.
• Linguofacial vein — drains tongue + face.
• Maxillary vein — drains deep face, jaws.
• Internal jugular vein — small or absent in horse; well-developed in dog.
• Vertebral vein — drains brain & cervical spine.

Lymph Nodes (Routine in Clinical Exam):
• Parotid LN — caudal to mandible.
• Mandibular LN — at angle of mandible.
• Lateral & Medial Retropharyngeal LN — deep, behind pharynx.
• Superficial Cervical (Prescapular) LN — base of neck, on supraspinatus.
• Deep Cervical LN — chains along carotid (cranial, middle, caudal).

Detailed anatomy

VEINS AND LYMPH NODES

The external jugular is formed by major tributaries including linguofacial and maxillary veins and drains through species-specific venous convergence into the brachiocephalic/cranial-caval system. Mandibular, parotid and superficial cervical nodes may be examined externally; medial retropharyngeal nodes are deep and are not routinely externally palpable. Thoracic-duct and right-lymphatic drainage patterns vary among species and should not be forced into a fixed human-style venous-angle scheme.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

External jugular dominant; internal jugular tiny. Lower jugular furrow has carotid lying just deep — risk during venepuncture.

Cattle

Routine examination includes accessible mandibular, parotid and superficial cervical regions; medial retropharyngeal nodes are deep. Bovine TB: caseous granulomas in retropharyngeal LN.

Dog

Mandibular LN palpable in normal dogs. Generalized lymphadenopathy = lymphoma.

Clinical relevance

Tuberculosis Inspection: Mandatory examination of mandibular, retropharyngeal, mediastinal, mesenteric LN at meat inspection. Lymphadenopathy in dog: Mandibular + prescapular + popliteal LN palpation = lymphoma screen.
