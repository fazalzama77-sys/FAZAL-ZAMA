Veterinary anatomy overview

Sphenoid complex:
• Presphenoid and basisphenoid form the cranial base and part of the orbit.
• In ruminants and pigs, orbital fissure and foramen rotundum unite as the foramen orbitorotundum.
• Cattle lack an alar canal and possess a distinct foramen ovale for V3.
• Horse and dog retain separate orbital fissure and foramen rotundum; both possess an alar canal.

Detailed anatomy

SPHENOID COMPLEX

The presphenoid and basisphenoid form much of the cranial base and contribute to the orbit. In ruminants and pigs, the orbital fissure and foramen rotundum unite as the foramen orbitorotundum, transmitting CN III, IV, VI, V1 and V2. In the horse and dog those openings remain separate. Cattle lack an alar canal; horse and dog possess one. The bovine foramen ovale transmits V3, whereas in the horse V3 exits through the oval notch/foramen-lacerum region.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Foramen orbitorotundum present; alar canal absent; foramen ovale distinct.

Horse

Orbital fissure and foramen rotundum remain separate; alar canal present; V3 exits through the oval notch/foramen-lacerum region.

Dog

Orbital fissure, foramen rotundum and foramen ovale are separate; alar canal present.

Pig

Foramen orbitorotundum is present, so this fusion is not unique to ruminants.

Clinical relevance

Functional Context: The absence of the Alar Canal in the ox means the maxillary artery courses on the surface of the pterygoid muscles rather than through a bony conduit, altering the approach for vascular ligation in this region compared to the horse.
