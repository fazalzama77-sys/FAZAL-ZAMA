Veterinary anatomy overview

Scroll-like Bones in Nasal Cavity:
• Increase surface area for warming/filtering air
• Dorsal Nasal Concha: Single scroll (from 1st endoturbinate)
• Ventral Nasal Concha: Double scroll (dorsal + ventral rolls) - with species-specific scrolls; the horse also has defined dorsal and ventral conchal scrolls
• Middle Concha: Smaller, between dorsal and ventral
• Highly vascular mucosa
• Ox: a species-variable ethmoturbinate arrangement; fixed universal counts should not be taught total

Detailed anatomy

NASAL CONCHAE

Dorsal and ventral nasal conchae project from the lateral nasal wall and bear spiral lamellae whose arrangement is species-specific. Ethmoidal conchae occupy the caudal nasal cavity. In the horse, dorsal and ventral conchae contain defined dorsal and ventral scrolls and communicate with paranasal compartments in specific ways; they must not be described elsewhere as simply single-scroll structures.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Dorsal and ventral conchae are well developed; conchal sinuses and communications are species-specific.

Horse

Dorsal and ventral conchae have spiral lamellae/scrolls and defined conchal sinuses.

Dog

Complex conchae and ethmoturbinates provide a large olfactory surface.

Fowl (Gallus domesticus)

Usually three nasal conchae with avian morphology, not mammalian scroll terminology.
