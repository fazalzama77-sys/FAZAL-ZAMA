Veterinary anatomy overview

Lacrimal Bone:
• Lacrimal bulla: Unique thin-walled protuberance inside orbit containing sinus
• Clinical: Fragile - damage during enucleation causes sinusitis
• Large facial plate, extensive on face
• Nasolacrimal canal entrance on orbital surface

Zygomatic Bone (Malar):
• Forms ventral orbital margin, rostral zygomatic arch
• Complete bony orbit: Frontal process joins frontal bone (unlike dog/pig)
• Extensive facial surface reaching facial tuberosity

Detailed anatomy

Lacrimal Bone (Os lacrimale)

Located at the anterior margin of the orbit, articulating with the frontal, zygomatic, and maxillary bones.

Anatomical Topography

Lacrimal Bulla (Bulla lacrimalis): A unique, thin-walled bony protuberance inside the orbit of the ox. It is fragile and contains the lacrimal sinus, a posterior extension of the maxillary sinus. Damage to this bulla during enucleation can lead to sinus infection.

Facial Plate: Large and flat on the face, extending rostrally.

Orbital Plate: Forms the medial wall of the orbit.

Nasolacrimal Canal: The entrance (fossa) leads to the duct carrying tears to the nose. In the ox, it is situated on the orbital surface.

Zygomatic Bone (Os zygomaticum / Malar)

Forms the ventral margin of the orbit and the rostral part of the zygomatic arch.

Anatomical Topography

Facial Surface: Extensive in the ox, extending rostrally to the facial tuberosity.

Frontal Process: Projects dorsally to articulate with the zygomatic process of the frontal bone. This articulation completes the bony orbit caudally, a key distinction from carnivores where the orbit is open.

Temporal Process: Extends caudally to meet the zygomatic process of the temporal bone, forming the zygomatic arch.

Comparative anatomy

Ox (Bos taurus)

Lacrimal: Bulla present (large, fragile). Facial part large. Lacrimal fossa near orbital rim. Zygomatic: Complete orbit (joins frontal). Bifurcated (frontal/temporal procs). Large/broad.

Horse (Equus caballus)

Lacrimal: Absent bulla. Small facial part. Funnel-like; inside orbit. Zygomatic: Complete orbit. Blade-like. Large.

Dog (Canis lupus)

Lacrimal: Absent bulla. Very small (mostly orbital). On orbital rim. Zygomatic: Incomplete orbit (ligamentous closure). Curved bar. Small.

Pig (Sus scrofa)

Lacrimal: Absent bulla. Small. Two foramina usually. Zygomatic: Incomplete orbit (ligamentous closure). Robust. Massive.

Fowl (Gallus domesticus)

An avian lacrimal element is present and participates in the orbital framework; drainage anatomy differs from mammals. Zygomatic: Incomplete orbit (jugal bone). Rod-like (jugal). Very thin/rod-like.

Clinical relevance

Clinical Warning: The lacrimal bulla in the ox is thin-walled and easily damaged during surgical procedures such as enucleation (eye removal). Damage can lead to sinusitis as the bulla connects to the maxillary sinus system.
