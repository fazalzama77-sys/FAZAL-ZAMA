Veterinary anatomy overview

High-yield points:
• Most mammals, including ox, horse, dog and pig, have seven cervical vertebrae; rare mammalian exceptions exist.
• C1 is the atlas and C2 the axis.
• C3-C5 are typical cervical vertebrae; C6 has a characteristic ventral lamina and C7 marks the cervicothoracic transition.
• Atlas foramina and vertebral-artery routes vary by species.

Detailed anatomy

CERVICAL VERTEBRAE

Most mammals, including the principal domestic mammals, have seven cervical vertebrae; rare mammalian exceptions exist. The atlas has dorsal and ventral arches and species-specific alar, lateral and transverse foramina. The axis bears the dens. Vertebral-artery and spinal-nerve routes through the atlas vary by species and should not be generalized. Domestic fowl usually have 14 cervical vertebrae and marked neck mobility, but owl rotation must not be used as the chicken comparison.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Seven cervical vertebrae; atlas foramina and axis morphology are bovine-specific.

Horse

Seven cervical vertebrae with large transverse processes and species-specific atlas vascular routes.

Dog

Seven cervical vertebrae; relatively greater neck mobility than large ungulates.

Fowl (Gallus domesticus)

Usually 14 cervical vertebrae in the chicken, permitting extensive but not owl-like neck rotation.

Clinical relevance

Atlanto-Axial Subluxation (Toy Dogs): Yorkie / Toy Poodle puppies — congenital absence or fracture of the dens → atlas slips on axis → spinal cord compression → tetraparesis. Surgical fixation needed. Cervical IVDD (Dog): Disc herniation between C2-C7 — neck pain + thoracic-limb lameness. Wobbler Syndrome (Horse, Great Dane, Doberman): Caudal cervical instability with cord compression — ataxia. CSF Tap Sites: Atlanto-occipital (cisterna magna) — needle on dorsal midline between occiput and wings of atlas. Vertebral Artery Damage: Trauma to wings of atlas can rupture the vertebral artery as it passes through the alar foramen.
