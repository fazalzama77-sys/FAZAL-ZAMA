Veterinary anatomy overview

Comparative exits:
• Ox and pig: foramen orbitorotundum combines orbital fissure and foramen rotundum.
• Horse and dog: orbital fissure and foramen rotundum remain separate.
• Ox: foramen ovale carries V3 and no alar canal is present.
• Horse: V3 uses the oval-notch/foramen-lacerum region and the alar canal is present.
• Dog: separate foramen ovale and alar canal.

Detailed anatomy

MAJOR CRANIAL EXITS

Ruminants and pigs possess a foramen orbitorotundum formed by fusion of the orbital fissure and foramen rotundum. Horse and dog retain separate openings. Cattle lack an alar canal; horse and dog possess one. The bovine mandibular nerve uses the foramen ovale, while the equine nerve exits through the oval notch/foramen-lacerum region. CN IX-XI use the jugular foramen or species-equivalent caudal lacerum region, and CN XII uses the hypoglossal canal.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Foramen orbitorotundum; distinct foramen ovale; no alar canal.

Horse

Separate orbital fissure and foramen rotundum; alar canal present; V3 exits at the oval notch/foramen-lacerum region.

Dog

Separate orbital fissure, rotundum and ovale; alar canal present.

Pig

Foramen orbitorotundum present.

Clinical relevance

Critical Localization: Complete ophthalmoplegia (paralysis of all eye muscles) with sensory loss to the cornea and upper face in cattle localizes to the Foramen Orbitorotundum region. In horses, such diffuse deficits would suggest a cavernous sinus syndrome or multiple lesions rather than a single foramen issue.
