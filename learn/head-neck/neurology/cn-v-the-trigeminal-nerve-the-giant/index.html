Veterinary anatomy overview

Trigeminal nerve: V1 is sensory to the cornea, orbit and dorsomedial face; V2 supplies the maxillary teeth, upper lip and adjacent face; V3 supplies mandibular sensation and motor fibers to masticatory muscles. In cattle, qualified lacrimal/zygomaticotemporal communications form the cornual pathway; avoid labeling it simply as V2.

Detailed anatomy

TRIGEMINAL NERVE

V1 is sensory to the orbit, cornea and dorsomedial facial structures; V2 supplies the maxillary teeth, upper lip and related facial regions; V3 supplies mandibular sensation and branchial motor fibers to masticatory muscles. The supraorbital foramen transmits the supraorbital nerve with accompanying vessels. In adult cattle the rostral epidural rete is supplied chiefly by maxillary arterial rete branches; vertebrobasilar supply should be described separately. Cornual innervation must follow the qualified ophthalmic-associated description used in the introductory entry.

Comparative anatomy

Ox (Bos taurus)

Foramen Ovale is discrete, separate opening in basisphenoid wing. Principal bovine cornual supply follows the zygomaticotemporal pathway; accessory innervation can occur. Rete Mirabile present.

Horse (Equus caballus)

V3 exits via 'Oval Notch' in Foramen Lacerum, not a discrete foramen. No Rete Mirabile—internal carotid artery persists.

Dog (Canis lupus)

Standard mammalian pattern with distinct Foramen Ovale. Alar canal transmits maxillary artery.

Goat (Capra hircus)

CRITICAL: Dual innervation of horn—Cornual nerve (V2) AND Infratrochlear cornual branch (V1). Requires two-site blockade.

Clinical relevance

The 'Dropped Jaw' Test: Bilateral mandibular nerve paralysis causes the jaw to hang open with inability to close the mouth (motor deficit). In unilateral lesions, the jaw deviates toward the affected side when the mouth is opened due to unopposed contralateral pterygoid contraction.
