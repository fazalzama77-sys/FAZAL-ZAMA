Veterinary anatomy overview

Pulmonary circuit: Right ventricle → pulmonary trunk and arteries → pulmonary capillaries in the lungs → pulmonary veins → left atrium. The right atrium receives systemic venous return before blood enters the pulmonary circuit through the right ventricle.

Systemic circuit: Left ventricle → aorta → systemic vessels → systemic veins → cranial and caudal venae cavae → right atrium. The left atrium receives pulmonary venous return before blood enters the systemic circuit through the left ventricle.

Aortic-arch and terminal branches vary among domestic species. In cattle, a single brachiocephalic trunk arises from the arch and gives rise to both subclavian arteries and the bicarotid trunk.

Detailed anatomy

SYSTEMIC AND PULMONARY CIRCULATION

The pulmonary circuit carries blood from the right ventricle to the lungs and back to the left atrium; the systemic circuit carries blood from the left ventricle through the aorta and returns it to the right atrium. Aortic-arch branching is species-specific: cattle have a single brachiocephalic trunk that gives both subclavians and a bicarotid trunk. Aortic termination into external iliac, internal iliac and median sacral pathways also varies by species. Venous valves and lymphatic drainage are not identical in every vessel or species. Thoracic-duct and right-lymphatic drainage should be described with veterinary species variation rather than a fixed human pattern.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Single brachiocephalic trunk arises from the aortic arch; cerebral arterial supply includes a maxillary-derived rete.

Horse

Aortic arch branching and terminal vessels follow the equine pattern; heart-to-body ratio varies with breed and training.

Bird

The right systemic aortic arch persists in birds, and the heart has four chambers.

Clinical relevance

Clinical relevance: Patent ductus arteriosus produces a continuous murmur and is one of several congenital cardiac defects in dogs; it should not be declared universally the most common without population context.
