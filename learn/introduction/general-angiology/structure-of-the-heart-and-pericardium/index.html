Veterinary anatomy overview

Heart = 4-chambered muscular pump in the middle mediastinum.

4 chambers: Right atrium → Right ventricle → (lungs) → Left atrium → Left ventricle.

4 Valves:
• Tricuspid (right atrioventricular) — between R atrium & R ventricle.
• Pulmonary semilunar — exit of R ventricle.
• Mitral / Bicuspid (left AV) — between L atrium & L ventricle.
• Aortic semilunar — exit of L ventricle.

3 Layers of Wall:
• Epicardium = visceral pericardium (outer).
• Myocardium = thick cardiac muscle (middle).
• Endocardium = inner endothelial lining.

Pericardium = double-walled fibrous sac:
• Fibrous pericardium (outer).
• Parietal serous pericardium (inner).
• Pericardial cavity has small amount of fluid.

Detailed anatomy

HEART AND PERICARDIUM

The heart has right and left atria and ventricles, atrioventricular valves and aortic and pulmonary valves. The number and arrangement of pulmonary venous ostia, valve cusps, chordae and wall-thickness ratios vary among species and individuals. The pericardium consists of a fibrous layer and parietal and visceral serous layers. Sternopericardiac or phrenicopericardiac attachments are species-specific. Adult cattle commonly have ossa cordis in the cardiac fibrous skeleton.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Heart lies chiefly between approximately the third and sixth ribs/intercostal spaces and commonly contains ossa cordis.

Horse

Cardiac size varies with body size, breed and training; avoid anecdotal fixed weights or chord formulas.

Dog

Cardiac silhouette is assessed radiographically with conformation-aware methods such as vertebral heart size.

Clinical relevance

Auscultation Sites (Cow): Left side — Mitral (5th ICS, lower third), Aortic (4th ICS, mid-third), Pulmonic (3rd ICS, mid-third). Right side — Tricuspid (3rd-4th ICS, mid-third). Pericardiocentesis: Aspiration of pericardial fluid in tamponade. Hardware Disease (cattle): Reticular foreign body penetrates pericardium → septic pericarditis ('washing-machine' heart sounds).
