Veterinary anatomy overview

Palpable bovine bony landmarks: scapular cartilage at the withers, tuber coxae, tuber sacrale, tuber ischiadicum, olecranon, calcaneal tuber, accessible vertebral processes, mandible and facial bones.

Lymph nodes: Accessibility is species- and patient-dependent. Mandibular, parotid, superficial cervical, subiliac and superficial inguinal/supramammary nodes may be examined where superficial. Medial retropharyngeal nodes are deep and are not routine external palpation sites; the popliteal node is readily assessed in dogs but is not a universal large-animal landmark.

Routine arterial pulse sites: equine facial artery at the mandibular border, canine/feline femoral artery at the medial thigh and bovine median caudal artery on the ventral tail. The common carotid is deep and must not be promoted as an ordinary superficial pulse site in the jugular furrow.

Detailed anatomy

PALPABLE LANDMARKS

Palpable bones, superficial lymph nodes and pulse sites differ among species. Mandibular, parotid, superficial cervical, subiliac and superficial inguinal nodes may be assessed where accessible. Medial retropharyngeal nodes are deep and are not routinely palpated externally or “per nasal.” Pulse sites include the bovine median caudal artery, equine facial artery and canine/feline femoral artery. Lymph-node enlargement is an important finding but is not by itself diagnostic of bovine leukosis or lymphoma.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Coccygeal pulse + jugular venepuncture = standard.

Horse

Facial artery pulse + jugular vein. Digital pulse at fetlock — increased = laminitis.

Dog

Femoral pulse + cephalic vein — primary clinical sites.

Clinical relevance

Daily clinical exam = 'TPR' (Temperature, Pulse, Respiration) + LN palpation + body condition score using palpable bony points.
