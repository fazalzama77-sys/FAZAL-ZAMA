Veterinary anatomy overview

Anatomy = the science of the structure of the body and the relationship between its parts. (Greek: ana = up + tome = cutting → 'cutting up').

Main Branches Studied in B.V.Sc:
• Gross (Macroscopic) Anatomy — naked-eye study (in dissection hall).
• Microscopic Anatomy = Histology — tissue level under microscope.
• Cytology — cell-level structure.
• Embryology / Developmental Anatomy — formation from fertilization to birth.
• Comparative Anatomy — across species (ox vs horse vs dog vs fowl).
• Surface (Topographic) Anatomy — palpable structures on the live animal.
• Applied (Clinical) Anatomy — surgical/diagnostic application.
• Radiographic Anatomy — interpretation of X-ray/USG/CT/MRI.

Detailed anatomy

VETERINARY ANATOMY

Veterinary anatomy studies the structure and relationships of animal bodies and applies them to clinical examination, surgery, imaging, reproduction and pathology. Major branches include gross anatomy, histology, cytology, developmental anatomy, comparative anatomy, surface anatomy, applied anatomy and imaging anatomy. Practical study may use ethically sourced cadavers, prosections, plastinated specimens, models and validated digital resources. Exact student-to-cadaver ratios, body-donation deadlines or mandatory species rules must be cited to a current VCI/MSVE regulation rather than stated without a source.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Reference species for VCI syllabus — students must know ox anatomy first, then compare.

Horse

Carlo Ruini wrote the first species-specific veterinary anatomy book (1598).

Dog

Standard small-animal model for clinical anatomy.

Fowl

Avian anatomy (poultry science) is integrated into the same VCI course.

Clinical relevance

Curricular use: Learn the reference species and the prescribed comparative species in the current institutional and VCI/MSVE syllabus. Do not present an uncited fixed “five-species rule” as a universal regulation.
