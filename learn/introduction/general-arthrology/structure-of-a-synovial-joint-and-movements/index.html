Veterinary anatomy overview

Components of a typical synovial joint:
1. Articular cartilage — hyaline; smooth gliding surface; avascular.
2. Joint capsule — fibrous outer + synovial inner.
3. Synovial membrane — secretes synovial fluid.
4. Synovial fluid — lubricant + nutrient.
5. Ligaments — collateral, intracapsular, extracapsular.
6. Menisci / Discs (where present).

Movements at synovial joints:
• Flexion / Extension — decrease/increase angle.
• Adduction / Abduction — toward/away from midline.
• Rotation — around long axis.
• Circumduction — combined motion describing a cone.
• Pronation / Supination — rotate forearm/leg.
• Gliding — sliding of flat surfaces.

Detailed anatomy

SYNOVIAL JOINT

Articular hyaline cartilage covers subchondral bone and is nourished chiefly by synovial fluid. The capsule has an outer fibrous layer and inner synovial membrane. Type A synoviocytes are macrophage-like and type B synoviocytes synthesize hyaluronan and lubricating components. Ligaments, menisci, discs, fat pads, bursae and tendon sheaths occur according to the joint. Movements include flexion, extension, rotation, abduction, adduction and gliding within species-specific limits.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Pronation and supination are essentially absent because the radius and ulna are functionally fixed; this suits a limb specialized for parasagittal locomotion.

Dog

Appreciable pronation and supination are retained because the radius can rotate relative to the ulna; the range is less than in humans and varies with conformation.

Cat

The shoulder is a spheroidal synovial joint. Feline forelimb mobility is enhanced by the muscular attachment of the scapula to the trunk, a reduced clavicle and compliant periarticular soft tissues.

Clinical relevance

Sprain = stretched/torn ligament. Strain = stretched/torn muscle/tendon. Luxation = dislocation (bone out of joint). Subluxation = partial luxation. Effusion = excess synovial fluid (visible swelling).
