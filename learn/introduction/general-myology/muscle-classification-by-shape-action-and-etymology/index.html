Veterinary anatomy overview

By Shape:
• Fusiform — spindle-shaped (biceps).
• Triangular — fan-shaped (pectorals).
• Quadrilateral — square (rhomboideus).
• Strap-like — long, parallel (sartorius, sternohyoideus).
• Pennate — feather-like:
– Unipennate (one side).
– Bipennate (both sides).
– Multipennate (many).
• Circular (sphincter) — ring (orbicularis oculi).

By Action:
• Flexor / Extensor — flex / extend joint.
• Adductor / Abductor.
• Sphincter / Dilator.
• Levator / Depressor.
• Rotator (medial / lateral).
• Tensor.

Muscle Naming (Etymology): Latin/Greek roots indicating shape, location, action, attachments, or number of heads.

Detailed anatomy

MUSCLE CLASSIFICATION

Muscles may be fusiform, strap-like, convergent, circular or pennate and are named for shape, location, attachments or action. Names do not guarantee the same architecture in every species: canine and feline biceps brachii have a single head of origin despite the historical name. Domestic-animal terminology should use cranial/caudal rather than human anterior/posterior where applicable. The equine reciprocal apparatus belongs to the hindlimb and links stifle and hock through fibularis tertius and superficial digital flexor.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Stay apparatus = cooperative ligament+muscle system that lets the horse sleep standing (key UG concept).

Dog

Masticatory-muscle form varies with skull conformation and breed. A universal claim that brachycephalic dogs possess hypertrophied pterygoids specifically 'for bite strength' is unsupported.

Bird

Pectoralis major (downstroke) and supracoracoideus (upstroke) are the chief flight muscles.

Clinical relevance

Atrophy = wasting of muscle (denervation, disuse). Hypertrophy = increase in fibre size (training, anabolic agents). Contracture = permanent shortening (post-trauma fibrosis). Myositis = inflammation.
