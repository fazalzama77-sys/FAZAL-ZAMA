Veterinary anatomy overview

Sacrum (Ox):
- 5 sacral vertebrae fused into a single triangular bone.
- Forms the roof of the pelvis.
- Articulates with ilium (sacroiliac joint).
- Sacral foramina (4 dorsal + 4 ventral pairs) for sacral nerves.
- Sacral canal continues the vertebral canal; routine bovine caudal epidural access is at the sacrocaudal or first intercoccygeal space.

Coccygeal (Caudal) Vertebrae:
- Number variable: ~18-20 in ox, ~18 in horse, 20-23 in dog, 20-23 in pig.
- First few resemble lumbar; rest become simple rod-like bones.
- Lose spines, transverse processes, and arches caudally.
- Hemal arches/processes on ventral surface of cranial coccygeal vertebrae (Cy3-Cy4 in ox) — protect ventral coccygeal artery (used to feel pulse in cattle).

Detailed anatomy

SACRUM

In cattle the sacrum is normally formed by five fused sacral vertebrae and transmits weight between the vertebral column and ilia. Its base bears the promontory; its apex joins the first caudal vertebra; auricular surfaces articulate with the ilia; dorsal and ventral sacral foramina transmit branches of sacral nerves.

The sacral canal continues the vertebral canal and contains meninges and cauda-equina nerve roots. The cauda equina is not the caudal end of the spinal cord; it is the bundle of roots caudal to the conus medullaris. In adult cattle the conus may extend to the lumbosacral/S1 region, which is clinically important.

Caudal vertebral number and reduction vary. Hemal processes/arches protect ventral caudal vessels in proximal tail segments. Standard bovine caudal epidural access is at the sacrocaudal or first intercoccygeal space, identified by careful palpation and performed using species-appropriate technique.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Sacrum has 5 vertebrae too, but spinous processes are SEPARATE (median crest is interrupted). Coccygeal ~18.

Dog

Sacrum has 3 fused vertebrae only. Coccygeal vertebrae 20-23 (long tail).

Pig

Sacrum 4 vertebrae. Coccygeal ~20-23.

Fowl

Sacrum + last thoracics + lumbars + first coccygeals all fuse into a single bone called the SYNSACRUM. Last 4-6 caudal vertebrae fuse into PYGOSTYLE (supports tail feathers).

Clinical relevance

Caudal Epidural Anaesthesia (cattle): Local anaesthetic injected into the epidural space at the S5-Cy1 or Cy1-Cy2 junction. Used for: obstetric procedures, vaginal/rectal/perineal surgery, prolapse correction. A correctly selected caudal epidural desensitizes caudal sacral/coccygeal territories according to its segmental spread — anus, vulva and tail become numb but the cow stays standing.
Cranial Epidural: More cranial epidural spread can weaken the hindlimbs; approach and dose require patient-specific calculation and monitoring. Used for major hindlimb/udder surgery.
Tail bleeding (coccygeal venipuncture): Standard route to draw blood from cattle — needle inserted on the ventral midline of tail at Cy3-Cy4 level into the ventral coccygeal vessel.
