Veterinary anatomy overview

Large irregular bone of the pelvic girdle.

Ox standard: ilium, ischium and pubis unite around the acetabulum; a small acetabular bone also contributes during development before fusion.

Palpable landmarks: tuber coxae (hook), tuber sacrale and tuber ischiadicum (pin).

Other features:
- Acetabulum: socket for the femoral head, with lunate surface, fossa and notch.
- Obturator foramen: largely closed by the obturator membrane/muscles; the obturator nerve and vessels leave through the obturator canal at its cranial margin.
- Pelvic symphysis: union of right and left pubis and ischium.

Detailed anatomy

THE HIP BONE — OS COXAE

Each os coxae consists principally of ilium, ischium and pubis, which unite around the acetabulum; a small acetabular bone contributes during development. The paired hip bones meet at the pelvic symphysis and articulate with the sacrum at the sacroiliac joints.

Palpable bovine landmarks: tuber coxae, tuber sacrale and tuber ischiadicum.

Acetabulum: Its lunate surface articulates with the femoral head; the acetabular fossa is non-articular and the notch interrupts the rim.

Obturator region: The large foramen is occupied mainly by obturator membrane and muscles. The obturator nerve and vessels pass through the obturator canal at the cranial margin rather than freely through the entire opening.

Sex differences: Female pelvic dimensions are generally more favorable for parturition, but shape varies with breed and individual.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Pelvis narrower; acetabulum deeper. Tuber coxae very prominent (point of hip).

Dog

Smaller, more horizontal pelvis. Common site of HIP DYSPLASIA — shallow acetabulum + loose hip → arthritis.

Pig

Wide flat ilium; tuber coxae less obvious.

Fowl

Os coxae fused with synsacrum (sacrum + lumbar + caudal vertebrae). Pubic symphysis OPEN in females (allows large egg passage). Renal fossa lodges kidneys.

Clinical relevance

Pelvimetry: Measuring pelvic inlet in heifers before first calving — narrow inlet predicts dystocia.
Calving paralysis (downer cow): Compression of the obturator nerve as it crosses the floor of pelvis during difficult calf delivery → cow cannot rise; legs splay sideways.
Hip Dysplasia (Dog): Shallow acetabulum → loose hip joint → painful arthritis in large breeds.
