Veterinary anatomy overview

Ox Metatarsus:
- Cannon Bone = fused Mt III + Mt IV (just like forelimb cannon).
- Slightly LONGER and more 4-sided than metacarpal cannon (way to tell front from back!).
- Vestigial Mt II + Mt V usually small or absent.

Digits (Ox):
- 2 functional digits (III & IV) — cloven hoof.
- 2 dewclaws (digits II & V) — non-weight-bearing.
- Each functional digit has 3 phalanges (P1-P2-P3) + proximal sesamoids (2) + distal sesamoid (navicular) (1).
- Each P3 lies within one of the two claws; together the claws form the cloven hoof (os ungulare).

Hindfoot vs Forefoot of Ox:
- Hind cannon LONGER & more square in cross-section.
- Front cannon shorter & rounder.

Detailed anatomy

METATARSUS — CANNON BONE OF HINDLIMB

Like the forelimb, the bovine metatarsus is the fused Mt III + Mt IV = 'cannon bone'. The fused bone has a dorsal longitudinal groove showing where the two original bones met. Distally it splits into two trochleae for the two digits.

How to tell hindlimb cannon from forelimb cannon:
• Hindlimb (metatarsal) cannon: LONGER, more SQUARE in cross-section.
• Forelimb (metacarpal) cannon: SHORTER, more ROUNDED.

Vestigial Mt II and Mt V (the splint-like bones) are usually small buttons or absent.

DIGITS — same plan as forelimb:
• 2 functional digits (III, IV) — together they form the cloven hoof.
• 2 dewclaws (II, V) — small, non-weight-bearing nubs above and behind the fetlock.
• Each functional digit has:
– P1 = proximal phalanx (long pastern bone)
– P2 = middle phalanx (short pastern bone)
– P3 = distal phalanx (coffin bone / os ungulare) — encased in the hoof horn
– 2 proximal sesamoids at the fetlock
– 1 distal sesamoid (navicular) behind P3

HOOF: The horn capsule covering P3. Each functional digit has its own claw → bovine 'cloven hoof'.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

1 functional digit (Mt III). Mt II + Mt IV are SPLINT BONES. P3 is the coffin bone with retrossal processes; navicular bone present behind it.

Dog

4 functional digits (II-V). Hind dewclaws (digit I) often present in some breeds and may be removed surgically. P3 = ungual crest (claw).

Pig

4 digits on hindlimb — III & IV main, II & V smaller but reach the ground on soft surfaces.

Fowl

TARSOMETATARSUS = single fused bone (distal tarsals + Mt II, III, IV). Usually 4 toes (3 forward + 1 back = anisodactyl). Spur present in cocks on medial side of tarsometatarsus.

Clinical relevance

Foot rot (interdigital phlegmon) in cattle: Acute bacterial infection of interdigital skin and deeper soft tissues.
Sole ulcer: A circumscribed sole-horn lesion associated with corium damage; it is not a synonym for laminitis.
Laminitis: Diffuse laminar/corium disease with multifactorial pathogenesis.
Navicular disease (horse): A podotrochlear syndrome most often affecting forelimbs.
IV regional anesthesia: Local anesthetic is placed in a superficial distal limb vein under a tourniquet using aseptic clinical technique.
