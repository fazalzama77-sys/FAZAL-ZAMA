Veterinary anatomy overview

Internal iliac system: Supplies pelvic walls, pelvic viscera, gluteal region, perineum and external genital structures through species-variable caudal gluteal, internal pudendal, umbilical and urogenital branches. External pudendal vessels instead arise through the deep-femoral/pudendoepigastric pathway and pass through the inguinal region.

Detailed anatomy

PELVIC ARTERIES

In cattle the internal iliac commonly continues through a caudal gluteal/internal pudendal pattern. Urogenital branches supply bladder, rectum and reproductive organs; internal pudendal supplies perineal and genital structures. Cranial gluteal supply has a species-specific origin. External pudendal vessels arise from the deep femoral/pudendoepigastric system and accompany the inguinal canal, so they must not be merged with internal-pudendal supply.

Comparative anatomy

Cattle

Long common caudal gluteal/internal pudendal pattern is characteristic.

Horse

Obturator and genital arterial contributions differ from cattle.

Dog

Internal iliac branching differs and should be learned from canine-specific maps.

Clinical relevance

Application: Pelvic surgery, castration and perineal procedures require identification of internal versus external pudendal pathways.
