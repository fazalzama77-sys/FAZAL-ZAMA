Veterinary anatomy overview

Important Lymph Nodes (palpable / clinical):

1. Subiliac LN (= Prefemoral LN):
- Largest external LN of hindlimb.
- Located in front of thigh, in the fold of flank.
- EASILY PALPABLE in cattle.
- Drains: skin of thigh, abdominal wall, flank.

2. Popliteal LN:
- Behind stifle (in popliteal fossa).
- Drains: distal hindlimb (foot, leg).
- Routinely palpable in dogs and cats but deep and not normally palpable in cattle.

3. Superficial Inguinal LN:
- In groin region.
- In males: scrotal lymph nodes drain scrotal coverings; the testes drain to lumbar (aortic) nodes.
- In females: supramammary (mammary) LN (drain udder) — clinically important in mammary inflammation and neoplasia, though enlargement is not disease-specific

4. Deep Inguinal / Iliac LN: along iliac arteries; drain pelvic viscera & deep hindlimb.

5. Sacral / Internal Iliac LN: pelvic floor; drain rectum, vagina, bladder.

Detailed anatomy

LYMPH NODES OF HINDLIMB AND PELVIS

The subiliac node is readily palpable in cattle and drains superficial flank/thigh regions. The popliteal node drains the distal limb and is palpable in dogs and cats but normally deep and not routinely palpable in cattle. Superficial inguinal nodes drain superficial inguinal, scrotal or mammary tissues according to sex; the testes drain to lumbar (aortic) lymph nodes, not scrotal nodes. Mammary lymph drainage is species- and gland-dependent: canine glands can drain toward axillary or superficial inguinal pathways. Deep inguinal, iliac and sacral nodes drain deeper pelvic/hindlimb structures. Node enlargement supports localization but is not disease-specific.

Comparative anatomy

Cattle

Subiliac and supramammary LNs are routinely examined. Persistent enlargement → lymphosarcoma (BLV).

Dog

Popliteal & superficial inguinal LN are palpable in standard physical exam — enlargement = lymphoma.

Clinical relevance

Mastitis: Supramammary-node enlargement or tenderness may support inflammation but does not confirm mastitis; combine udder examination with milk testing/culture as indicated.
Bovine lymphoma: Generalized lymphadenopathy may involve subiliac and mammary nodes.
Distal limb infection: Regional lymph nodes can react, but absence or presence of enlargement is not diagnostic alone.
