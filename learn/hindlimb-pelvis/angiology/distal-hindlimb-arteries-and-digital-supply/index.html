Veterinary anatomy overview

Distal pathway: Cranial tibial becomes dorsal pedal at the tarsus and continues into dorsal metatarsal arteries. Perforating tarsal/metatarsal branches join plantar arches. In cattle, common digital arteries divide into axial and abaxial proper digital arteries for digits III and IV.

Detailed anatomy

DISTAL HINDLIMB VASCULATURE

Dorsal pedal and dorsal metatarsal vessels communicate with medial/lateral plantar and plantar-metatarsal systems through perforating branches. Bovine terminal vessels serve two principal digits, while the equine pattern supplies a single digit through medial and lateral plantar digital arteries and an intraosseous terminal arch. Use bovine axial/abaxial terminology rather than copying the equine map.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Two functional digits receive axial and abaxial proper digital arteries.

Horse

Dorsal metatarsal and plantar systems supply the single digit and terminal arch.

Dog

Dorsal and plantar metatarsal branches supply multiple digits.

Clinical relevance

Clinical relevance: Digital perfusion and pulse findings must be interpreted with species-specific anatomy and the lesion site.
