Veterinary anatomy overview

High-Yield Points:
• Rectum = terminal part of the colon, lies in pelvic cavity dorsal to bladder/genital tract.
• Cranial part is peritonealized; caudal part (~last 15 cm) is retroperitoneal — clinically important for safe rectal palpation.
• Anal canal: short terminal segment with two sphincters → internal anal sphincter (smooth, involuntary) + external anal sphincter (striated, voluntary, supplied by pudendal nerve).
• Anus: external opening, surrounded by skin (anocutaneous line at junction).
• Site for caudal epidural anaesthesia (S5-Cy1) — paralyses anal sphincter for procedures.

Detailed anatomy

Rectum and Anus

The rectum is the terminal part of the large intestine, lying in the pelvic cavity dorsal to the urogenital organs and ventral to the sacrum. It runs roughly straight (hence 'rectum'). The cranial portion is suspended by the mesorectum and covered by peritoneum; the caudal ~15 cm becomes retroperitoneal — this section can be torn during rough rectal palpation, leading to fatal peritonitis.

The anal canal is the very short terminal segment lined by stratified squamous epithelium. It is surrounded by two sphincters:
• Internal anal sphincter: smooth muscle, involuntary, autonomic control.
• External anal sphincter: striated muscle, voluntary, innervated by the caudal rectal nerves (branches of pudendal nerve) — paralysis after sacral fractures or epidural overdose.

Anus: The external opening, closed by the sphincters and surrounded by hairy skin. The mucocutaneous junction is called the anocutaneous line.

Vasculature & Innervation: Caudal rectal artery (from internal pudendal); innervation as above. The pelvic plexus on the lateral wall of the rectum carries parasympathetic fibres governing defecation.

Comparative anatomy

Cow

Long, accessible per rectum — routine for pregnancy diagnosis, palpation of pelvic organs.

Horse

Wall very thin and easily torn during rectal exam — major cause of euthanasia in clinical practice.

Dog

Short rectum. Anal sacs (paranal sinuses) lie at 4 and 8 o'clock — common site of impaction/abscess.

Fowl

No true anus — rectum opens into the cloaca, which has urodeum, coprodeum, proctodeum compartments.

Clinical relevance

Rectal tear (horse): Grade 1-4 based on depth — Grade 4 (full thickness) is life-threatening. Anal sac disease (dog): Impaction or infection — scooting behaviour. Caudal Epidural: S5-Cy1 site — anaesthesia for prolapse correction, dystocia, perineal surgery. Rectal Prolapse: Common in piglets, lambs after coughing/diarrhoea/parturition.
