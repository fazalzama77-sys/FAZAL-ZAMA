Veterinary anatomy overview

Plantar side: Tibial nerve divides into medial and lateral plantar nerves. Their common/proper digital branches supply the plantar foot; a deep branch contributes plantar metatarsal nerves.
Dorsal side: Superficial and deep fibular branches contribute dorsal metatarsal and digital nerves. Branching differs between the single equine digit and paired bovine digits.

Detailed anatomy

DISTAL HINDLIMB INNERVATION

Near the hock the tibial nerve divides into plantar nerves, while the common fibular system supplies dorsal territories. In cattle, dorsal and plantar common-digital nerves divide into axial and abaxial proper-digital branches for digits III and IV. In horses, medial/lateral plantar nerves and plantar metatarsal nerves are targeted at defined levels in diagnostic analgesia. Always name the nerve and level rather than using 'plantar block' without qualification.

Comparative anatomy

Ox

Paired digits require axial and abaxial digital-nerve terminology.

Horse

Single functional digit has medial/lateral plantar digital nerves and plantar metatarsal branches.

Dog

Dorsal and plantar metatarsal/digital branches serve multiple digits.

Clinical relevance

Blocks: Use species-appropriate anatomy, calculated dose and sterile technique. A high four-point block is an equine metatarsal-level block, not a generic tibial block.
