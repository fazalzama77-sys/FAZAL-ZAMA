Veterinary anatomy overview

Cranial gluteal nerve: Supplies middle/deep gluteal muscles and tensor fasciae latae.
Caudal gluteal nerve: Supplies superficial gluteal/gluteobiceps components according to species.
Caudal cutaneous femoral nerve: Sensory to caudal thigh/perineal-adjacent skin.
Pelvic nerves: Parasympathetic supply to distal colon, rectum, bladder and genital organs; distinct from the somatic pudendal nerve.

Detailed anatomy

GLUTEAL AND PELVIC NERVES

Gluteal nerves pass through the greater sciatic region to their muscle groups and are important in hip stability and propulsion. Caudal cutaneous femoral and cluneal branches map the rump/caudal-thigh skin. Pelvic splanchnic nerves carry parasympathetic fibers to pelvic plexuses, whereas hypogastric nerves carry sympathetic fibers and pudendal nerves provide somatic perineal motor and sensory supply. This autonomic-somatic distinction is essential for bladder, rectal and genital function.

Comparative anatomy

Cattle

Obstetric trauma can affect several lumbosacral branches, not only the obturator nerve.

Horse

Gluteal and sciatic-region topography is important for injections and pelvic trauma.

Dog

Pelvic fractures and surgical approaches can injure gluteal, sciatic or autonomic pathways.

Clinical relevance

Localization: Combine gait, muscle atrophy, perineal reflexes, tail tone and bladder function to distinguish somatic from autonomic injury.
