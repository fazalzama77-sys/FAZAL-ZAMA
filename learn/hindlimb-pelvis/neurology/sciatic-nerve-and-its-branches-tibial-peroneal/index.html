Veterinary anatomy overview

SCIATIC NERVE (L6-S2): LARGEST nerve of the body.
- Exits pelvis via greater sciatic foramen, passes caudal to greater trochanter, runs down caudal thigh.
- Splits at stifle into:

1. TIBIAL NERVE (caudal branch):
- Runs down BACK of leg (with gastrocnemius).
- Supplies: hamstrings + GASTROCNEMIUS + SDF + DDF.
- Damage → cannot extend hock; 'dropped hock'.
- BLOCK SITE: Medial side of the hock just above the calcaneus.

2. PERONEAL (FIBULAR) NERVE (cranial branch):
- Wraps around lateral side of stifle (head of fibula) — VULNERABLE!
- Runs down FRONT of leg.
- Supplies: tibialis cranialis + long digital extensor (hock flexors).
- Damage → cannot lift foot; toe drags → 'knuckling'.
- BLOCK SITE: Lateral side of leg, just below head of fibula.

Detailed anatomy

SCIATIC, TIBIAL AND COMMON FIBULAR NERVES

The sciatic nerve courses caudal to the hip and divides at a species-variable level into tibial and common fibular nerves. The tibial nerve supplies caudal crural muscles and continues through plantar branches; the common fibular nerve divides into superficial and deep branches for cranial/lateral crural structures. Injury patterns differ accordingly. In equine diagnostic analgesia, a high four-point block targets the medial/lateral plantar nerves and plantar metatarsal nerves in the proximal metatarsus; it is not a tibial nerve block at the hock. Distal plantar blocks are described by their exact nerve and level.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Tibial, common-fibular and distal plantar blocks are distinct techniques performed at defined species-specific levels.

Cattle

Peroneal nerve compression on hard concrete floors causes knuckling — common 'downer cow' complication.

Dog

Sciatic damage from pelvic fractures or wrong-site IM injection in caudal thigh.

Clinical relevance

UG SYLLABUS NERVE BLOCKS — hindlimb sites:
• Tibial Block: a species-specific medial crural/hock landmark selected from a current procedural reference.
• Peroneal Block: a species-specific lateral proximal-crural landmark selected from a current procedural reference.
• Plantar Block: the exact medial/lateral plantar or plantar-metatarsal nerves at the intended level.
Peroneal Paralysis (cattle): Cow recumbent on concrete → toe drags when trying to walk. Pad floors well.
