Veterinary anatomy overview

Pudendal nerve: Sacral nerve contribution supplying perineal and external-genital structures through several branches.

Pudendal block: A specialist large-animal technique requiring species-specific landmarks and asepsis; it must not be taught as one universal needle point or fixed volume.

Caudal epidural: Commonly performed at the sacrocaudal or first intercoccygeal space in cattle to desensitize tail/perineum while preserving standing ability when correctly dosed.

Cranial epidural: Produces more cranial spread and possible hindlimb motor blockade. Drug, concentration and volume must be calculated for species, body weight, desired spread and patient status.

Detailed anatomy

PUDENDAL NERVE AND EPIDURAL ANESTHESIA

The pudendal nerve and its perineal, caudal rectal and dorsal genital branches supply the perineum and external genitalia. Pudendal blockade in the bull is an advanced technique; landmarks, approach, agent and volume vary and require a veterinary procedural reference.

Epidural anesthesia places drug in the epidural space. A correctly dosed caudal epidural at the sacrocaudal/first intercoccygeal space can desensitize the tail and perineum while preserving stance. Greater cranial spread can cause hindlimb weakness or recumbency. Never substitute a blanket volume: calculate drug and volume from species, body weight, concentration, desired segmental spread and patient condition. Lumbosacral access also carries species-specific spinal-cord risk, particularly in adult cattle where the conus may extend caudally.

Comparative anatomy

Bull

Pudic nerve block is the standard way to relax penis for examination/surgery.

Mare

Caudal epidural analgesia can be used for selected obstetric/perineal procedures; agent and volume are calculated for the individual mare and desired spread.

Dog

Lumbosacral epidural anesthesia may be used for selected caudal procedures by trained clinicians with weight-based dosing and monitoring.

Clinical relevance

UG SYLLABUS — Hindlimb/Pelvic Surgical Sites:
• Pudendal block: An advanced bovine technique whose approach and effectiveness require current species-specific guidance.
• Caudal epidural: Usually sacrocaudal or first intercoccygeal, with drug and spread calculated to preserve standing when intended.
• Cranial epidural: Greater cranial spread can produce hindlimb motor blockade and requires a separate patient-specific plan.
• Patellar Desmotomy: Cut medial patellar ligament for upward fixation of patella in horse.
• Urethrotomy: Open the urethra surgically to remove uroliths (calculi) — usually at sigmoid flexure in male cattle (where stones lodge).
• Castration / Vasectomy: Performed via scrotal/inguinal approach.
