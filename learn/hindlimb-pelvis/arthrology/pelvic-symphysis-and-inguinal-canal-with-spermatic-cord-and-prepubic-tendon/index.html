Veterinary anatomy overview

Pelvic symphysis: Cartilaginous in young animals and progressively ossifies; the fused adult symphysis does not loosen before parturition.

Inguinal canal: Oblique caudoventral passage. Its deep ring is bounded mainly by the caudal internal abdominal oblique and inguinal ligament; its superficial ring is in the external abdominal-oblique aponeurosis.

Spermatic cord contents: ductus deferens, testicular artery, pampiniform venous plexus, lymphatics and autonomic nerves. Cremaster and vaginal tunics are associated coverings, not vascular contents.

Prepubic tendon: Strong attachment of abdominal wall structures to the cranial pubic brim.

Detailed anatomy

PELVIC SYMPHYSIS, INGUINAL CANAL AND PREPUBIC TENDON

The pelvic symphysis unites the paired pubis and ischium. It ossifies with age and does not physiologically reopen at calving; increased pelvic compliance comes from ligaments and soft tissues.

The inguinal canal extends between a deep ring bounded mainly by the caudal internal abdominal oblique and inguinal ligament and a superficial ring in the external abdominal-oblique aponeurosis. Contents vary with sex and species and include the spermatic cord in males, vaginal process/round ligament in females, external pudendal vessels and genitofemoral nerve.

The spermatic cord contains ductus deferens, testicular artery, pampiniform venous plexus, lymphatics and nerves. Cremaster and vaginal tunics are associated coverings. The prepubic tendon anchors the abdominal floor to the cranial pubis and can rupture in late-gestation mares.

Comparative anatomy

Stallion

Wide inguinal canal — INGUINAL HERNIA more common.

Mare

Prepubic tendon RUPTURE risk near foaling, especially with twins.

Bull

Symphysis ossifies early — rigid pelvis (hence dystocia common in heifers).

Clinical relevance

Castration: Scrotal incision → spermatic cord identified → emasculator crushes & cuts cord above testis. Bloodless (Burdizzo) method crushes cord externally without cutting skin.
Vasectomy: Small skin incision over cord → ductus deferens isolated and a section removed → animal sterile but retains testosterone (used for 'teaser' bulls).
Inguinal Hernia (stallion, boar): Loop of intestine slips through internal inguinal ring → emergency surgery if strangulated.
Prepubic Tendon Rupture: Pregnant mare's belly drops dramatically; medical emergency.
