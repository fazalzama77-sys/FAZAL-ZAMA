Veterinary anatomy overview

Sacroiliac joint: A very low-motion articulation with a ventral synovial component and strong dorsal fibrous/ligamentous support. It transmits axial load to the pelvis. Dorsal and ventral sacroiliac ligaments and the sacrosciatic ligament stabilize the region. Greater and lesser sciatic openings and their contents differ among domestic species and must be listed species-specifically.

Detailed anatomy

SACROILIAC JOINT

The auricular surfaces of ilium and sacrum form a joint with a ventral synovial portion and a strongly reinforced dorsal fibrous portion. Dorsal/ventral sacroiliac and sacrosciatic ligaments transmit weight and restrict motion. In the horse the broad sacrosciatic ligament helps define greater and lesser sciatic foramina; in cattle the arrangement and structures crossing the notches differ, so one shared contents list is misleading. Prominent or asymmetric tubera sacrale ('hunter's bump') may reflect conformation or prior sacroiliac-region injury and is not by itself proof of subluxation.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Asymmetric tubera sacrale ('hunter's bump') may reflect conformation or prior sacroiliac-region injury and do not by themselves prove subluxation.

Cattle

Sacroiliac and sacrosciatic soft tissues become more lax near calving under hormonal influence; the adult fused pelvic symphysis does not reopen.

Clinical relevance

Pelvic Ligament Relaxation (Cattle): Just before calving, sacroiliac and sacrosciatic ligaments soften under hormone (relaxin) → tail head sinks → important sign of imminent parturition.
