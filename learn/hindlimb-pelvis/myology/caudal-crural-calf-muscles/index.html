Veterinary anatomy overview

Back of leg — extensors of hock + flexors of digits:

1. Gastrocnemius (2 heads — medial & lateral) — biggest; tendon = common calcaneal tendon → tuber calcanei.
2. Superficial Digital Flexor (SDF) — wraps around the tuber calcanei (forms 'cap'); flexes digits.
3. Deep Digital Flexor (DDF) — has 3 heads; tendon goes around the medial side of hock; flexes digits.
4. Popliteus — small triangular muscle behind stifle.

Function: EXTEND the hock + FLEX the digits → 'push-off' in walking.

Nerve: TIBIAL nerve — branch of sciatic.

Detailed anatomy

CAUDAL CRURAL MUSCLES

Gastrocnemius extends the tarsus; superficial and deep digital flexors flex the digits and may act on the tarsus; popliteus flexes/rotates the stifle slightly. The common calcaneal tendon is not formed by only gastrocnemius and superficial digital flexor: depending on species it also receives contributions from biceps femoris, semitendinosus and gracilis. The equine superficial digital flexor is the caudal component of the reciprocal apparatus. The tibial nerve supplies the caudal crural group.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Calcaneal tendon and SDF cap on tuber calcanei extremely strong; part of stay apparatus.

Dog

Same plan; common rupture of common calcaneal tendon in working dogs.

Clinical relevance

Tibial Nerve Paralysis: Animal cannot extend the hock → 'dropped hock' posture; fetlock knuckles forward.
Rupture of the common calcaneal tendon (Gastrocnemius rupture): Occurs with species- and age-specific trauma or musculotendinous injury; the hock drops to the ground (plantigrade stance).
