Veterinary anatomy overview

Iliopsoas (psoas major + iliacus): arises from lumbar vertebral and iliac regions and inserts on the lesser trochanter; it flexes the hip.

Tensor fasciae latae: arises near the tuber coxae, inserts into fascia lata and acts on hip/stifle through the fascia.

Psoas minor: lies ventral to lumbar vertebrae and inserts on the iliopubic eminence/psoas minor tubercle; it flexes the lumbosacral region/pelvis.

Detailed anatomy

HIP FLEXORS

These muscles bring the limb FORWARD (= flexion of hip = protraction).

1. ILIOPSOAS:
The combined muscle of two parts:
• Psoas major — from bodies and transverse processes of last thoracic + lumbar vertebrae.
• Iliacus — from ventral surface of ilium.
Both join into a single tendon and insert on the lesser trochanter of femur.
Function: The MAIN flexor of the hip. Pulls the femur forward → essential for advancing the limb in walking/running. Also flexes the lumbar spine.

2. TENSOR FASCIAE LATAE:
From the tuber coxae (hook bone), runs down the side of the thigh, and inserts into the broad fascia of the thigh (fascia lata). When it contracts, it tenses the fascia and indirectly extends the stifle (because the fascia connects to the patella). It also helps flex the hip.

3. PSOAS MINOR:
A smaller, more medial muscle. From ventral lumbar vertebral bodies, inserting on the iliopubic eminence/psoas-minor tubercle. Functionally minor — flexes pelvis on the spine.
Note: Psoas and iliacus occupy the sublumbar region and have clinically important relations to lumbar nerves and retroperitoneal structures.

UG MEMORY: Iliopsoas → 'lesser trochanter, hip flexor, lumbar plexus'.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Iliopsoas powerful — needed for swinging the heavy hindlimb forward.

Dog

Psoas minor very small; psoas major large.

Clinical relevance

Clinical topography: Psoas major and iliacus lie along the sublumbar region and may be involved in retroperitoneal or iliopsoas injury; assess them using species-appropriate examination and imaging.
