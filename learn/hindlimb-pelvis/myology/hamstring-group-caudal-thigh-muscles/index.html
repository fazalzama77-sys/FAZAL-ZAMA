Veterinary anatomy overview

3 Big muscles on caudal thigh:

1. Biceps Femoris — most LATERAL; from tuber ischiadicum & sacrum.
2. Semitendinosus — middle, longer & rope-like.
3. Semimembranosus — most MEDIAL; broader.

Origins are chiefly ischial/sacral, but insertions differ: biceps spreads to patellar/crural fascia, tibia and calcaneal structures; semitendinosus reaches tibia and the common calcaneal tendon; semimembranosus reaches caudomedial femur and medial tibia. Actions on hip and stifle depend on limb position.

Nerve: Tibial part of Sciatic nerve.

Detailed anatomy

CAUDAL THIGH (HAMSTRING) MUSCLES

Biceps femoris, semitendinosus and semimembranosus share important ischial origins but have distinct insertions. Biceps femoris spreads to patellar/crural fascia, tibia and calcaneal structures; semitendinosus inserts on the tibia and contributes to the common calcaneal tendon; semimembranosus inserts on the caudomedial femur and medial tibia and does not join the common calcaneal tendon. Their actions on hip and stifle depend on limb position and weight bearing. They receive branches derived mainly from the tibial component of the sciatic nerve.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Same 3 muscles, very powerful — drive the gallop.

Dog

Same; well-developed; visible easily in lean dogs.

Clinical relevance

Sciatic Nerve Injury: Damages tibial portion → hamstrings cannot contract → animal cannot extend hip or flex stifle properly.
Penetrating injuries of caudal thigh can damage hamstrings → severe lameness.
