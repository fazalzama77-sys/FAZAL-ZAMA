Veterinary anatomy overview

Type: Muscles of the rump/croup — muscles with species- and position-dependent actions on the hip.

4 Gluteals in Ox:
1. Superficial Gluteus — small in ox; often partly fused with biceps femoris ('gluteobiceps').
2. Middle Gluteus — LARGEST and most powerful; on top of croup.
3. Deep Gluteus — under middle gluteus.
4. Accessory Gluteus — slip of middle gluteus.

Insertion: Greater Trochanter of femur (Middle gluteus to top, deep to a separate facet).

Function: EXTEND the hip joint = propel the body forward (powerful kick / jump).

Nerve: Cranial & Caudal Gluteal nerves.

Detailed anatomy

GLUTEAL MUSCLES

The superficial, middle, deep and accessory gluteal components vary by species. In cattle the superficial gluteus blends with biceps femoris as gluteobiceps. Middle gluteus is a powerful hip extensor inserting on the greater trochanter; deep gluteus is chiefly an abductor and may act according to limb position. Cranial gluteal nerve supplies middle/deep gluteal and tensor fasciae latae; caudal gluteal branches supply superficial gluteal/gluteobiceps as appropriate. Intramuscular injection sites must follow current species-specific clinical and food-animal guidance; the gluteal region should not be presented as a universally preferred site.

Comparative anatomy

Horse

Middle gluteus is HUGE in horses (extends forward over lumbar region) — the most powerful extensor for galloping.

Dog

Middle gluteus moderate; superficial gluteus small. All 3 are present.

Clinical relevance

IM injection in cattle/horse rump: Intramuscular injection sites must follow current species, product and food-animal guidance; the gluteal region is not universally preferred. Avoid the sciatic nerve which lies caudal to greater trochanter.
